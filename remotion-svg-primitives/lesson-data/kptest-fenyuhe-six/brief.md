# kptest-fenyuhe-six

**Audience.** 一年级 (~6–7 years old), Mandarin-speaking children. Narration in Chinese.
**Length.** 60–90s band — HINT for scope only, not a target to pad to.
**Builds on.** kptest-fenyuhe-five (5的分与合) — the child already knows the 分/合 routine for 5.
**Style.** default

## Knowledge point
The child leaves able to do **6的分与合** — 6 splits into 1和5, 2和4, 3和3 (and the symmetric pairs), and combines back into 6. Extends the part–whole pattern to a new number, including the equal split 3和3.

## The one beat
Six dots split into two groups — 1和5, then 2和4, then 3和3 — and 合 back each time. The 3和3 equal split is the highlight: the child watches the whole break exactly in half and recombine.

## Out of scope
No other numbers (5 is the prior section; 7+ are later). No +/−/= symbols. No counting past six. ONLY 6的分与合.

## Continuity
Reuse the registered dot / part–whole primitives; the six dots are identity-invariant across the splits. Reuse the 分/合 motion vocabulary established for 5的分与合 so the child recognizes the routine.

## Narration notes
Chinese only. NAME 分/合; the picture delivers the parts. Acquisition target — model the three splits as co-equal beats (none starved), give a learner-response wait-time gap ("6可以分成几和几?"), and a spaced-recall recap. Highlight the symmetric 3和3.
