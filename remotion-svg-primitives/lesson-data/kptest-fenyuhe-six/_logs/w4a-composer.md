## KEY DECISIONS

1. **Identity-invariant dot group: 6 React `<UnitBlock variant="dot">` instances, never destroyed.** The scene creates them ONCE in the render of `KptestFenyuheSixLessonScene` and interpolates their `x` per-frame from `mainDotPositionsForLayout(previousLayout)` → `mainDotPositionsForLayout(targetLayout)` across the cue's motion window. Recap sub-beats are a SEPARATE 18-dot group (3 sub-beats × 6 dots) in a RecapSpotlight — structurally three side-by-side snapshots, not the main six dots in a different layout. The visual-design §6 identity invariant is preserved for cues 1–8; the recap carries the same dot TYPE + COLOR + SIZE across all three sub-beats, with `<RecapSpotlight currentHighlight={i}>` driving dim-vs-active opacity.

2. **Recap mechanic: `<RecapSpotlight currentHighlight={i}>` per the W3b gap-scan, NOT a hand-roll.** The visual-design §4 originally prescribed "a hand-rolled per-cue color override" with a hedge: "if the W3b gap-scan ships a `RecapSpotlight` primitive, that's its call." W3b's gap-scan resolved the hedge in §4: `RecapSpotlight` exists in the catalog with the exact useWhen ("Drives 6的分与合's recap (1+5 → 2+4 → 3+3, the live highlight follows the spoken item)"). The composer passes `recapSubBeatNodes` (six dots per beat in their X+Y partition) and a `ringCenter` derived from `getRecapBeatX(activeIndex) + RECAP_BEAT_Y`. The `activeIndex` advances 0→1→2 at `RECAP_BEAT_ONE_AND_FIVE_FRAMES` / `+ RECAP_BEAT_TWO_AND_FOUR_FRAMES` boundaries — 3|3 lands LAST, highlight preserved. The previous sub-beat dims to 0.32 (the catalog's `dimOpacity`); the live ring draws a transient stroke at the active sub-beat's ringCenter.

3. **One `<PopIn motion="bouncy">` entrance: cue 5's "三和三" bond glyph ONLY.** `early-childhood-visual-taste` reserves the bouncy accent for the lesson's ONE introduction of a new concept. cue-split-3of3 is the lesson's new property (3和3 is new to 5's routine). Every other entrance is `<LabelCallout progress=…>` without PopIn — the bond glyph is held through the cue-relative window with fade-in/fade-out on the `BOND_OFFSET_BY_CUE[cueId]` + `BOND_DURATION_BY_CUE[cueId]` window, gated by the `(frame - from) / 12` and `(to - frame) / 6` clamps.

4. **Audio wiring is v4 cue-anchored, NEVER a continuous WAV.** The Complete wrapper mounts `<LessonAudioLayer voiceClips={kptestFenyuheSixVoiceClips} />` (the kit creates one `<Sequence from={clip.fromFrame}>` per clip — the resolve-mount is automatic). The bed uses `<LessonBgmLayer bed="math-calm-68-cmaj" windows={spansToWindows(voiceSpans)} totalFrames={…} />` — the duck windows derive from the per-cue clip spans, not from any hardcoded table. SFX are owned by the composer: `pop` on `cues["cue-announce-split-1of5"].startFrame + ANNOUNCE_TITLE_DURATION_FRAMES` (the bond "一和五" entrance), `ta-da` on `cues["cue-reveal-answer"].startFrame + Math.floor(REVEAL_ANSWER_THREE_THREE_DISPLAY_FRAMES / 2)` (the 3|3 reveal midpoint per visual-design §2 "single transient sunshine highlight at midpoint"). Intro sting (`mandarin-accent`) fires 2 frames after the lesson starts; outro resolve fires 6 frames before the recap's last sub-beat ends.

5. **Question prompt composition: THREE readable parts per `learner-response-gap` rule.** The cue-learner-response-gap cue (held silent ≥3s per pedagogy §8 acquisition floor, baked as `gap: { seconds: 4, reason: "learner-response" }` on the clip) renders (1) a localized prompt label "6可以分成几和几？" via `<LabelCallout fontSize={60}>` at 900-weight, (2) a `PulseCircle` ring (two concentric strokes, opacity modulated by `Math.sin(phase * Math.PI)` over 60f) drawing the eye, and (3) an `<IconAsset name="microphone" variant="color">` glyph to the left. The whole thing is held through the entire cue window (the silence is the SIGNAL — the affordance is a single held composition, not a fade). A bare low-opacity glow is FORBIDDEN per the `learner-response-gap` rule.

6. **Intro card sequencing per `announce-topic` `requires` ("title reads alone first") + W3b gap-scan §5 ("title fades across the next 18 frames as the bond-glyph '一和五' enters").** The title `<LessonIntroCard title="6的分与合" card={false} progress={titleProgress}>` is mounted ONLY in `titleStateAt(frame)` — which returns non-null only during the first `ANNOUNCE_TITLE_DURATION_FRAMES` (60f, 2.0s) of cue-announce-split-1of5. Inside the title's window: the card's `progress` ramps 0→1 over the first 12f (the LessonIntroCard's entrance stagger), holds at 1 for the middle, then the WRAPPER opacity ramps 1→0 across the last 18f (so the title fades out as the bond "一和五" enters at frame 60, and the two never visually overlap). The dots are NOT yet mounted during this window (they appear at `dotMountFrame = startFrame + 60 + 60`, i.e. AFTER the title's 60f and the bond's 60f). Sequencing: title alone (60f) → bond alone (60f) → dots separate into 1|5 (30f) → dwell (≥45f). No overlap of title + dots; no overlap of title + cast; no hard cut between title and bond.

7. **Bond glyph clears the dots' bbox.** The bond glyph is at `(BOND_GLYPH_X, BOND_GLYPH_Y)` in `ZONE_LABELS` (y=80..267) and the dot row is at `DOT_Y` in `ZONE_OBJECTS` (y=267..520). The zones are disjoint; the bond glyph NEVER overlays the dots. Even when both are simultaneously visible (cues 2–6 modeling + conservation), the visual is read as "label above, dots below" — no occlusion. (Verified per the manifest's collision math: `ZONE_LABELS` and `ZONE_OBJECTS` are disjoint rectangles in the y-axis, and the bond-glyph bbox is `BOND_GLYPH_TARGET_SIZE × BOND_GLYPH_TARGET_SIZE` centered in ZONE_LABELS — it cannot intersect the dot row's bbox.)

8. **Cue-learner-response-gap holds the WHOLE layout statically (no motion window).** The first-delivery scene crashed in the measured pass on this cue: `cue-learner-response-gap` is in `CUE_TARGET_LAYOUT` (target="WHOLE") but is NOT in `MOTION_WINDOWS` (the gap is held silent, no motion). The early-return check `if (!target) return null;` did not catch it because `"WHOLE"` is truthy, so the code fell through to `const motionStart = cue.startFrame + window.start;` and `window` was `undefined`. The fix: added a guard right after the window lookup — if `MOTION_WINDOWS[containingCueId]` is undefined, return the target layout with `opacity: 1` and no transition. The dots render statically in a single row through the entire ≥3s silent gap, which is the intended visual (the six dots are a reminder of "the whole" the child is asked to split).

9. **Zero frame literals in scene code.** Verified: every `frame` reference in the scene goes through a `cues[id].startFrame + <named layout.ts constant>` derivation. The only `frame` literals in the source are relative-comparison checks (`frame <= drawFrom`, `frame >= drawTo`, `frame <= from`, `frame < to` — clamp guards, not absolute timeline frames). `Math.floor(...)` is only used to round `REVEAL_ANSWER_THREE_THREE_DISPLAY_FRAMES / 2` (a layout constant), not to compute a frame from arbitrary time. The Complete wrapper uses `cues["cue-announce-split-1of5"].startFrame + INTRO_STING_OFFSET_FRAMES` (a 2-frame offset) and `cues["cue-spaced-recap-all-three"].startFrame + OUTRO_RESOLVE_OFFSET_FRAMES` (a 6-frame offset) — both named constants in the wrapper file, not literals.

10. **Named motion vocabulary (EASE.*) — zero raw `Easing.bezier` literals.** Easing references in scene: `EASE.inOutCubic` (default for splits/merges), `EASE.outQuint` (cue 5 climax only, per visual-design §3 "30f `EASE.outQuint` (3|3 split — only beat with this weight)"). No `Easing.bezier(...)` literals, no inline `{ damping, stiffness, mass }` spring configs. The `<Breathe>` wrap on the six-dot group uses `bpm={15} amplitudeScale={0.005} phaseSeed="kp6-dot-group"` — defaults per `CAPABILITIES.md#magic-fx-library`; the bond-glyph group uses `phaseSeed="kp6-bond-${cueId}"` so the same cue's bond doesn't sync with the dot group's breath.

11. **Lint-clean per the W4a contract.** ESLint on the 4 owned files returns EXIT 0. Confirmed: PascalCase `KptestFenyuheSixLessonScene` (a lowercase component name would fail `react-hooks/rules-of-hooks` — `useCurrentFrame()` / `useMeasureHook()` only work inside Capitalized components), ESM `import` only (no `require()`), no unused imports/vars (`colors`, `typography`, `video`, `interpolate`, `AbsoluteFill`, `EASE`, `PopIn`, `IconAsset`, `LabelCallout`, `LessonIntroCard`, `RecapSpotlight`, `TeacherMark`, `UnitBlock`, `Breathe`, `FXDefs`, `GlintFlash` — every import is used; `colors` is read for the cream background + dot color, `typography` for the fontFamily, `video` re-exported for the Complete wrapper's import).

12. **The manifest's `sixDotsBbox` and `recapBeatBbox` use the SAME interpolation as the scene.** The manifest imports the same `kptestFenyuheSixCues` array the scene reads, the same `ZONE_OBJECTS` / `DOT_Y` / `RECAP_DOT_SIZE` layout constants, and the same `RECAP_BEAT_*_FRAMES` motion-budget constants. If a future composer re-layouts the dots, the manifest and the scene drift together — `npm run lesson:check` will catch the drift if the manifest's bbox stops matching the scene's `getBBox()` at the same frame (the measured pass reads the live SVG bbox, the linear pass uses the manifest's bboxAt).

13. **Why the recap sub-beats are NOT in the main six-dot group's identity chain.** The recap is a SPACED-RECALL — three previously-taught sub-beats are now displayed side by side for the child to retrieve each in turn. They are three STATIC GROUPS, each a six-dot instance, not one group migrating through three layouts. Mounting the main six-dot group in the recap would either (a) keep it in the layout of cue 8 (SPLIT_3_3) and break the visual that says "these are 1|5, 2|4, AND 3|3" or (b) require cue-9-only logic in the main group's interpolation, which would over-complicate the state machine. The recap sub-beats are visually identical to the corresponding main layouts (same dot type, same color, same size, same partition) — the identity invariant is preserved at the per-sub-beat level, and the RecapSpotlight drives the dim/highlight mechanic.
