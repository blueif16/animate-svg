# fen-yu-he — pedagogy gate (Wave 0)

Source brief: `lesson-data/fen-yu-he/brief.md`. One paragraph per beat, three lines each
(`discovery` / `stage` / `focal`) per `lesson-pedagogy` skill §8. Audit at bottom (§9).

The knowledge point has four distinct cognitive moves, in this order:
(1) one whole can be split into two parts — and the same parts push back together into the
whole (分 / 合, inverse); (2) one split, frozen, can be *read* as a 分合式; (3) sliding one
object at a time generates the next split — the **ordered method**; (4) order matters —
(1,4) and (4,1) are two different splits, so 5 has exactly 4 ways. Each gets its own beat (or
beats), because each is a different mental act. The opening intro card announces the topic and
carries no discovery — it is structural (CLAUDE.md mandates it), not a teaching beat, and is
listed first only so the storyboard knows it exists.

## intro-title

discovery:  (no discovery — structural topic-announce card mandated by CLAUDE.md: lesson title + "5 的分与合" teaser; the child learns nothing new, it sets expectation.)
stage:      represent
focal:      the title text appearing — this is the only beat where text is the focal element; it announces, it does not teach.

## five-whole

discovery:  These five candies in the middle are one group of five — the whole we are about to take apart (a felt property: the child sees the intact "5" they will operate on, so the later split reads as *this* five coming apart, not new candies).
stage:      concrete
focal:      the five candies arriving/settling as one centered heap; nothing else on screen moves.

## split-into-two

discovery:  One line drawn down the middle splits the five candies into a left part and a right part — the same five, now in two heaps (new operation on a unit the child already has: a whole can be *divided* into two parts).
stage:      concrete
focal:      the dividing line appearing and the candies separating left/right; the candies themselves are unchanged, only the gap/line is new.

## recombine-inverse

discovery:  Push the two heaps back together and the line goes away — the same candies make five again, so splitting can be undone (a relation between the two parts and the whole: 分 and 合 are reverse moves on one set of candies).
stage:      concrete
focal:      the two heaps sliding back into one centered heap (the reverse of the previous beat); same candies, motion reversed.

## read-fen-he-shi

discovery:  A split can be written as a 分合式 — five on top, two slanting lines down to the two part-numbers — and read two ways: top-down "五分成 X 和 Y", bottom-up "X 和 Y 组成五" (new representation: the picture the child just watched now has a symbolic form they can read).
stage:      represent
focal:      the 分合式 diagram drawing in (the 5, the two strokes, the two part-numbers) above the candy heaps; this is the first stand-in for the concrete split, so it is the change.

## first-ordered-split

discovery:  Start the ordered hunt at the smallest left part — left 1, right 4 — and freeze it as the first 分合式 in a column (a new operation: instead of random splits, *begin at one on the left* and lock it down).
stage:      represent
focal:      the (1,4) split forming and its 分合式 settling into the top slot of a results column; the candy heaps show 1 | 4.

## slide-one-at-a-time

discovery:  Move exactly one candy from right to left and you get the next split — (1,4) → (2,3) → (3,2) → (4,1) — each slide producing one new 分合式 below the last (the ordered method: +1 on the left each time generates the complete sequence with nothing skipped).
stage:      represent
focal:      the single candy sliding across the divider, the left/right counts ticking 1→2→3→4 and 4→3→2→1, each frozen as the next row in the column.

## ordered-column-complete

discovery:  Read top to bottom and the four 分合式 climb 1,2,3,4 on the left — because we went in order, every split is here once, none missed, none repeated, so 5 has exactly four ways (the felt payoff of the ordered method: completeness is *visible* in the sorted column).
stage:      represent
focal:      the completed column of four 分合式 (held still) with the left-part numbers reading 1,2,3,4 down the side; the running "共 4 种" sense comes from the sorted order, not a new symbol.

## order-matters

discovery:  Left 1 right 4 and left 4 right 1 are two different rows in the column — swapping which side has more is a *different* split, not the same one (a relation: position/order distinguishes the two, which is why the count is four and not two).
stage:      represent
focal:      the top row (1,4) and bottom row (4,1) of the column highlighting in turn to show they are distinct entries; everything else in the column dims.

## Audit (§9) — adapted to fen-yu-he

1. **One discovery sentence per beat.** Each beat above has exactly one `discovery` line and one cognitive task. The two genuinely separate ideas inside "分与合" — *splitting* (`split-into-two`) and *undoing the split* (`recombine-inverse`) — are split into two beats rather than crammed into one, because "watch it come apart" and "watch it go back" are two mental acts (§5). Likewise *forming the first split* (`first-ordered-split`) and *generating the rest by sliding* (`slide-one-at-a-time`) are separate: starting-at-one is one decision, the +1 march is another.

2. **Every element serves a named discovery, not the adjacent math.** No `+`, `−`, `=` anywhere — the brief and §3 forbid the equation stage; only the 分合式 notation is used, and it first appears at `read-fen-he-shi` (stage `represent`), never before. There is no abstract "互逆" label or definition chip — the inverse is *shown* by `recombine-inverse` reversing `split-into-two` on the same candies (brief: 互逆通过同一组糖来回挪动来体现,不下定义). There is no "5 = 1+4" or set-theory framing; the only symbolic object is the 分合式.

3. **No beat reaches above its stage (§3).** `five-whole`, `split-into-two`, `recombine-inverse` are `concrete` — the child operates on real candies. The 分合式 and the results column are `represent` (a read-able stand-in for the split). Nothing reaches `symbolize`: no digit-only equation, no operator symbols, no abstract definition. The intro card is `represent` only because it is structural text, not a teaching object.

4. **Narration prepares or names; picture delivers (§4).** The count of splits (four) is delivered by the *sorted column the child watches build*, not announced up front — narration names the action ("再挪一个" / "下一种") while the slide delivers the next pair (brief: 强调有序时多用"再""又""下一种"). The 分合式 reading ("五分成一和四" top-down, "一和四组成五" bottom-up) names what the diagram already shows; the diagram delivers the structure. Narration must NOT say "一共有四种分法" before `ordered-column-complete` lets the child see the four rows.

5. **Focal element is the one that changes; supporting elements quiet (§6).** Each beat names a single changing focal (line appearing, heaps separating, heaps recombining, 分合式 drawing in, one candy sliding, column highlighting). Reserve the two allowed pulses for: the entry of the central teaching object (the first 分合式 at `read-fen-he-shi` / `first-ordered-split`) and the recap punctuation (`order-matters` highlight). The candy heaps stay still except on the beat where they are the focal.

6. **Spoken count matches visual count (§7).** During `slide-one-at-a-time` the left/right candy counts and the 分合式 part-numbers tick together with the mouth: 左 1 右 4 → 左 2 右 3 → 左 3 右 2 → 左 4 右 1. No number is said that the screen does not also show.

7. **ASR / narration constraints carried (brief Narration notes).** 分 and 合 are single-character low-confidence tokens — every beat that names them must use a companion word (分开/分成, 合起来/组成), never the bare character. All-Mandarin, no English; "5" is spoken 五. These are narration-wave constraints recorded here so audio-captions (Wave 2b) honors them; pedagogy only flags them.
