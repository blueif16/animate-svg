# kptest-classroom-objects

**Audience.** 三年级 (~8 years old), Mandarin-speaking children in their first English unit. Teacher narration is in Chinese; the target nouns are in English.
**Length.** 60–90s band — HINT for scope only, not a target to pad to.
**Builds on.** kp1-hello-greetings (the two kid characters + the meet routine).
**Style.** default

## Knowledge point
The child leaves able to name three classroom objects in English — **pencil**, **pen**, **ruler** — three CO-EQUAL target words, each met, modeled, echoed, and retrieved with comparable weight. (Three, not five: teach few words deeply; crayon/eraser belong to a later section.)

## The one beat
A hand lifts each object in turn out of a pencil-case and names it — "pencil" … "pen" … "ruler" — the child watches each object appear and hears its English name land ON it as it rises. A short recap then shows all three together and retrieves each name.

## Out of scope
No greetings, no names (§1.1 / §1.4). No crayon, eraser, school-bag, book (later sections). No colors, no counting. ONLY pencil / pen / ruler.

## Continuity
Reuse the two kid characters as the presenters. Reuse the registered `DialogueExchange` / object-reveal primitives and `ReadAlongHighlight`. Each object is a reused `IconAsset`, never one-off art.

## Narration notes
Chinese framing, one English target noun per reveal beat. Each noun LEADS its reveal beat so the word lands as the object appears — do not tail-bury it behind a long Chinese sentence. Three co-equal items → balance airtime across all three; none starved. The hardest to pronounce (ruler /ˈruːlə/, the /r/ and final schwa) gets the extra echo. A real wait-time gap on each "now you say it." Model warmly, no harsh drilling.
