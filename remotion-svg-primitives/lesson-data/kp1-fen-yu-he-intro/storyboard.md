# Storyboard — kp1-fen-yu-he-intro

Knowledge points bundled: **§1.4《分与合》KP1 + KP2 + KP3** — a whole comes apart (*分*) and joins back (*合*); the 分合式 diagram records that fact in notation; 5 has four such splits. The video exists to make the path from a concrete dot-split into the formal 分合式 diagram *visible as one continuous transformation*, then enumerate the remaining splits of 5 over the same diagram shape.

Total: 10 cues, ~112s with natural pacing (target band 100–130s). The load-bearing cue is `fenheshi-intro` — the moment a picture becomes a symbol. The two cues bracketing it (`he-name` → `fenheshi-intro` → `fenheshi-read`) carry the most narration weight; the KP3 enumeration cues (`five-1-4`, `five-3-2-and-4-1`) are quieter because the diagram shape is already known.

Identity continuity: the same 5 dots that split in `fen-show` are the same 5 dots that rejoin in `he-show`. The count labels "5", "2", "3" that snap on in `fen-name` are the *same glyphs* that migrate up into the 分合式 in `fenheshi-intro` — not redrawn, re-anchored. The diagram shape established by `fenheshi-intro` (whole on top, two short diagonal strokes to two parts below) is reused identity-preserved in `five-1-4` and `five-3-2-and-4-1`.

Visuals are described in terms of reusable primitive concepts only. Wave 2 (visual-design) choreographs moves and assigns zones; Wave 3 (primitive-builder) confirms or builds the 分合式 primitive.

## intro
- **Goal**: Plant the lesson's name and hint at the central transformation, in one quick beat — the child reads "this is about taking five apart and putting it back."
- **Beat (narration draft, Mandarin)**: 「今天我们来认识《分与合》。」 *(short, calm; no count announced)*
- **On-screen visual**: A tiny preview of the central move plays under the title card: a small row of 5 dots briefly separates into 2 + 3 then snaps back. Below this preview, the title "分与合" writes on. Section subtitle "5以内数" sits above the title in a quieter weight.
- **Estimated duration**: 6–8s.

## fen-show
- **Goal**: The child watches a single group of 5 dots come apart into two smaller groups, with nothing told about how many — the picture delivers the separation.
- **Beat (narration draft, Mandarin)**: 「看，我们把这一堆点点分开。」 *(narrator names the action 分开 — does NOT announce the resulting counts)*
- **On-screen visual**: A row of 5 identical dots sits centered. The leftmost 2 dots ease leftward; the rightmost 3 dots ease rightward, opening a clear gap in the middle. No count labels yet. No diagram yet. The motion is slow enough that the child can mentally count both sides.
- **Estimated duration**: 9–11s.

## fen-name
- **Goal**: Attach the name *分* and three count labels (5, 2, 3) to what the child just saw — the action acquires a name, the parts acquire numbers.
- **Beat (narration draft, Mandarin)**: 「五个点点，分成两堆 — 这边二个，那边三个。这样把它们分开，就叫做『分』。」 *(narrator names the action and binds the term 分; per §4, the picture has already delivered the count by the time these numbers are spoken — they are confirmations, not announcements)*
- **On-screen visual**: A small count chip "5" briefly highlights above the original (now-separated) row, then fades. Above the left cluster, a "2" snaps on; above the right cluster, a "3" snaps on. After both part-labels have landed, the character "分" writes on in the gap between the two clusters, with a small downward arrow connecting it to the visible separation.
- **Estimated duration**: 11–14s.

## he-show
- **Goal**: The child watches the same picture run backwards — the two clusters move back into one group of 5. Nothing new is named.
- **Beat (narration draft, Mandarin)**: 「现在反过来 — 两堆点点合到一起。」 *(narrator names the reverse action; no labels announced)*
- **On-screen visual**: The "分" label fades out. The "2" and "3" chips fade out (the "5" chip from earlier stays *absent*). The left 2-cluster eases rightward; the right 3-cluster eases leftward; they re-form the original row of 5 dots, identical in spacing and color to the start of `fen-show`. This is run-backwards, not a new graphic — same dots, reverse vectors.
- **Estimated duration**: 8–10s.

## he-name
- **Goal**: Attach the name *合* to the joining direction and make the inverse relationship explicit — same picture, opposite direction.
- **Beat (narration draft, Mandarin)**: 「两堆合到一起，又变成了一堆 — 这就叫做『合』。分和合，是一张图，两个方向。」 *(this is the only cue where the narration is allowed to name a relation explicitly, because no picture alone can show "two directions of the same thing")*
- **On-screen visual**: The reunited row of 5 dots holds. Above the row, a horizontal "5" label appears, with the "2" and "3" labels reappearing beneath it on each side — arranged in a wide count strip. A two-headed arrow (one head pointing left → toward separation, one head pointing right → toward joining) draws on across the strip. The character "合" writes on beside the right-pointing head; the character "分" re-attaches beside the left-pointing head — the two terms now share one strip.
- **Estimated duration**: 12–15s.

## fenheshi-intro
- **Goal** (load-bearing): The child watches the count labels "5", "2", "3" migrate from the dot-strip into a clean diagram shape — whole on top, two diagonal lines descending to two parts. The shape is named 分合式. A picture becomes a symbol.
- **Beat (narration draft, Mandarin)**: 「我们把刚才看到的，用一张小图记下来 — 上面写整体的『五』，下面写两个部分『二』和『三』，中间用两条短线连起来。这张图叫做『分合式』。」 *(narrator names the diagram parts in order; picture delivers the migration)*
- **On-screen visual**: The dot row dims to a quiet supporting layer. The "5" label lifts upward and centers itself at the top of a forming diagram. The "2" and "3" labels each travel down-and-outward into part positions below — left part and right part. Two short diagonal strokes draw on from "5" down to "2" (left) and from "5" down to "3" (right). After all strokes and numbers settle, the term "分合式" writes on as a small caption beneath the whole diagram.
- **Estimated duration**: 14–17s.

## fenheshi-read
- **Goal**: The child learns the diagram is bidirectional — read top-down it says "5 分成 2 和 3"; read bottom-up it says "2 和 3 组成 5".
- **Beat (narration draft, Mandarin)**: 「从上往下读 — 五分成二和三。再从下往上读 — 二和三组成五。」 *(two readings, in order, paced one after the other — never overlapping)*
- **On-screen visual**: The diagram from the previous cue holds in place. A thin highlight sweep descends from "5" → through the two lines → onto "2" and "3"; while it sweeps, the term "分成" briefly appears beside the diagram. Sweep clears. Then a second highlight sweep ascends from "2" and "3" → up the two lines → into "5"; while it sweeps, the term "组成" briefly appears. The two sweeps run *sequentially* (never together).
- **Estimated duration**: 12–15s.

## five-1-4
- **Goal**: The same 5 doesn't only split as 2 and 3 — it can also split as 1 and 4. The diagram shape is reused; only the part numbers change.
- **Beat (narration draft, Mandarin)**: 「五还可以这样分 — 一和四。」 *(short; no announcement of "another way to split" — the picture says that)*
- **On-screen visual**: The completed 分合式 from `fenheshi-read` slides leftward and dims to a "we've seen this one" state (reduced opacity, kept fully visible). A new 分合式 with the same shape (whole "5" on top, two short diagonal lines, two parts below) draws on to the right of it — parts "1" and "4" land in place. Above the new diagram, a quick 1-dot + 4-dot strip briefly appears beneath it as concrete backing (a thin echo of the dot-row from earlier, anchoring the symbol to the count), then fades to leave only the diagram.
- **Estimated duration**: 11–13s.

## five-3-2-and-4-1
- **Goal**: The remaining two splits arrive — (3,2) and (4,1) — completing four diagrams in a row. Five has four splits.
- **Beat (narration draft, Mandarin)**: 「还可以是 — 三和二，四和一。五一共有四种分法。」 *(narrator names the two new splits in order, then states the total count of splits — this is a summary line, allowed because the four diagrams are visibly on screen by the end)*
- **On-screen visual**: The two existing 分合式 (the (2,3) and (1,4) ones) hold at left. Two more 分合式 draw on to their right in sequence — first (3,2), then (4,1). All four sit in a single row, equally spaced, same shape, same color. After the fourth lands, a quiet underline writes on beneath all four, signaling "this set is now complete."
- **Estimated duration**: 14–17s.

## outro
- **Goal**: A quiet hold — the three discoveries of the lesson (分, 合, and 5 has four splits) co-located on one screen, no new content.
- **Beat (narration draft, Mandarin)**: 「分与合 — 五的分与合，一共四种。」 *(restates only what is visibly present; no new fact)*
- **On-screen visual**: A three-up layout: top-left, a tiny replay of the split-and-rejoin animation (the two-headed strip with "分" and "合"); top-right or right side, the four 分合式 diagrams of 5 in a compact row; bottom, the title line "5 的分与合" writes on. Everything holds for a beat before a gentle fade.
- **Estimated duration**: 8–10s.

## Primitive demands surfaced for Wave 3 (gap-scan)

Storyboard names visual demands; Wave 3 decides which existing primitives suffice and what (if anything) is genuinely new. The demands this storyboard places on Wave 3:

- A **row of countable dots** that can: cleanly split into two clusters with mid-row spacing, run that motion in reverse, and accept count chips above each cluster. Likely satisfied by an existing `CountableObject`/dot-row composition; Wave 3 confirms.
- A **fen-he diagram shape** — whole on top, two short diagonal strokes descending to two part numbers below, with stroke-revealable lines and part-number write-on. Brief flags this as the likely new primitive (`FenHeDiagram`). Wave 3 confirms or rejects in favor of composing `NumberCard` + a generic line/brace.
- **Multi-diagram horizontal layout** — four 分合式 in one row with consistent spacing and a "previously seen" dim state on the first. This is the lesson scene's composition responsibility, not a new primitive — Wave 3 should not invent a "FenHeRow" primitive.
- **Bidirectional sweep / highlight** over the diagram for `fenheshi-read` — Wave 3 should check whether existing sketch / highlight primitives (or a sweep prop on `FenHeDiagram`) suffice; this is a small visual flourish, not load-bearing.
- A **topic-intro card composition** — math-subject intro with a small live preview of the central move underneath the title. Brief flags existing literacy intro primitives may not fit; Wave 3 decides whether to compose existing intro pieces or ship a math-subject variant.

## ASR risk flags (input for Wave 2b audio-captions)

Brief's narration-notes section already calls out the single-character utterances "分" (fēn) and "合" (hé) as ASR-risk. This storyboard preserves the risk in two cues — `fen-name` and `he-name` — because the lesson's contract is to bind those exact terms to a thing the child just watched. Storyboard intentionally does NOT pre-rewrite the lines; it flags for Wave 2b:

- **`fen-name`** — first appearance of the single-character term "分". The current draft pairs it with a multi-character companion ("分开" + "分成") on the same beat, which should help alignment; Wave 2b decides whether the bound utterance is sufficient or whether the standalone "分" needs further padding (e.g. "这叫做『分』开" or repeating the bound form).
- **`he-name`** — first appearance of the single-character term "合". The current draft pairs it with "合到一起" before binding the bare "合", same mitigation pattern. Same Wave 2b decision.
- **`fenheshi-intro` / `fenheshi-read`** — repeated use of "分合式" (3-char compound) and "分成" / "组成" (2-char compounds). Lower ASR risk than the bare single chars, but Wave 2b should still confirm tokenization in the Mandarin ASR step.

Wave 2b owns the actual narration script. Storyboard's beats are intent + length + emotional posture only.
