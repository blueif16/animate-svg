# Pedagogy — kp1-fen-yu-he-intro

What the child knows walking in:
- Counting 1–5 by ones.
- Recognizing the written digits 1, 2, 3, 4, 5.
- Some informal experience of "I have 2 apples, my friend has 3."

What the child leaves knowing:
- A whole can be SEPARATED into two parts (*分*); two parts can be JOINED back (*合*). Same picture, opposite direction.
- There is a written diagram (分合式) that records a fen-yu-he: whole on top, two parts below, two short lines connecting them.
- 5 has four splits: (1,4), (2,3), (3,2), (4,1).

The video's job is to make those three facts *visible as a single chain of discoveries*, not three taped-together mini-lessons.

---

## Cues

### intro

discovery:  today's lesson has a name — 分与合 — and it is about taking a small group apart and putting it back together.
stage:      symbolize (title is a label)
focal:      the title "分与合" appearing under a tiny preview of the central transition.

Intro must be short (~6s). Anything longer and the child has not started learning yet.

### fen-show

discovery:  a whole group of five dots can come apart into two smaller groups.
stage:      concrete
focal:      the 5 dots physically separating into a left cluster (2) and a right cluster (3).

The split is delivered visually; the narrator names the *action* ("我们把它分开"), not the result. The child does the noticing that "there are now two groups." No number labels yet — those come next.

### fen-name

discovery:  the action you just watched has a name — *分* — and the parts have counts: 5 separated into 2 and 3.
stage:      represent
focal:      the count labels "5", "2", "3" snapping on; the word "分" attaches to the action.

This is the moment "分" gets bound to a thing the child just saw. Stage 2 because the labels stand in for the dots — same idea, different surface.

### he-show

discovery:  two parts can move back together and remake the whole — the same picture, run backwards.
stage:      concrete
focal:      the 2-cluster and 3-cluster sliding back together into one 5-cluster.

The reverse motion does the teaching. No new labels.

### he-name

discovery:  joining two parts back has a name — *合*. 分 and 合 are two directions of the same picture.
stage:      represent
focal:      a two-headed arrow (or two paired arrows) on the count strip "5 ↔ 2 和 3"; the word "合" attaches to the joining direction.

This cue earns its existence by making the inverse relationship explicit — same picture, opposite direction. Without this cue, KP1 is half-taught.

### fenheshi-intro

discovery:  there is a written shape that records what you just saw — top number is the whole, two short lines come down to the two part numbers. The shape is called 分合式.
stage:      symbolize
focal:      the lines drawing in beneath the "5", with "2" and "3" sliding into place underneath.

This is the load-bearing transition of the whole video — the moment a picture becomes a symbol. The previous cue must have already established what "5 separates into 2 and 3" looks like; this cue records it in notation.

### fenheshi-read

discovery:  the diagram can be read in two directions — top-down is "*5 分成 2 和 3*", bottom-up is "*2 和 3 组成 5*".
stage:      symbolize
focal:      a downward sweep over the diagram ("分成"), then an upward sweep ("组成"). The two sweeps animate one after the other, not simultaneously.

This cue earns its place because without it the child reads the diagram in only one direction and misses the inverse relationship that fen-name + he-name set up.

### five-1-4

discovery:  the diagram doesn't have to read 2 and 3 — the same five dots can also split as 1 and 4.
stage:      symbolize
focal:      a new 分合式 with whole "5", parts "1" and "4" appearing; the previous diagram dims to a "we've already seen this" state.

### five-3-2-and-4-1

discovery:  there are still more ways — 3 and 2, and 4 and 1. Five has four splits in total.
stage:      symbolize
focal:      two more 分合式 diagrams arrive — (3,2) and (4,1) — completing the row of four.

Treating (2,3) and (3,2) as different splits is the lesson's choice and matches the textbook; the child does *not* have to discover this rule on their own here. The narration names the symmetry but does not lecture about ordering — that's KP6 (next video).

### outro

discovery:  three things from today — 分, 合, and 5 has four splits — fit on one screen at the same time.
stage:      symbolize
focal:      a tidy three-up recap: a small split-and-rejoin animation (分/合), the four 分合式 for 5, the line "5 的分与合" beneath.

Outro is a hold — no new content. It exists so the child can see the three discoveries co-located before the screen fades.

## Audit checklist (every downstream subagent runs this against its own output)

1. Each cue corresponds to exactly one `discovery` sentence above.
2. No cue smuggles in arithmetic operators (+, −, =) or 6+ examples.
3. The narration names; the picture delivers. No cue has the narrator announcing the count the child is supposed to read.
4. Stage discipline: a `concrete` cue must not require the child to read a 分合式 they haven't been shown yet; a `symbolize` cue must have an immediate prior `concrete` or `represent` cue covering the same fact.
5. The 分→合 inverse is taught with TWO cues (fen-name + he-name) plus the bidirectional reading of the 分合式 (fenheshi-read). If the inverse only shows up in narration, redesign.
