# storyboard — kptest-count-to-two

A two-count math-insight lesson: the child first discovers the **action** of counting (two identical apples arrive one at a time, each tagged with the next number word in turn), then discovers **cardinality** (the last number spoken — 二 / "2" — names how many there are altogether, as one tag consolidates to stand for the whole group). Two teaching cues carry the whole lesson; a brief topic-intro opens it. Insight-shaped (§8), no acquisition floors — 一/二 are near-known Mandarin count words, not targets to acquire, so there are no choral/echo/wait-time beats. cue-2's end-recall re-voices the total and is the lesson's one reserved pulse (§6); it absorbs the recap role so the spine ships exactly one closing retrieval beat, not two.

## cue-1-count

- **discovery ref** — "The child discovers the ACTION of counting — two identical apples arrive one at a time, and each gets the next number word in turn (一, then 二), so counting means tagging each object with the next number."
- **stage** — concrete
- **teaching action(s)** — `count-on` (the act of tagging in turn IS the model; no separate `model-target-slow` — 一/二 are not acquisition targets).
- **narration beat (INTENT, no copy)** — voice the count action in turn: name the moment an apple arrives, then say the ordinal as it tags that apple — 一 landing on apple-one, then 二 landing on apple-two. The spoken count word MUST land in sync with the tag attaching (§7 count-match); each tag appears IN TURN, never both at once, so the child does the count action alongside the picture. (Mandarin count words are pedagogy §4/§7 territory — name the relation referentially; copy is W2b.)
- **required visual** — two identical countable objects (apples), placed one at a time; exactly one apple per spoken number, with the matching ordinal number-tag appearing ON that apple in sync with its count word; both apples identical so only the count sequence differentiates them; identity-invariant (each apple keeps its place once tagged). Read OFF `count-on.requires`: countables + the per-item tag, NOT a badge-and-highlight double for the same signal. **Gap flag (named demand for W3b):** a countables-plus-ordinal-tag capability — "two identical objects that land one-at-a-time and each receive an in-turn ordinal number tag in sync with the spoken count word." Not assumed present; W3b resolves against the live catalog.

## cue-2-cardinality

- **discovery ref** — "The child discovers CARDINALITY — the last number you say when counting (二 / "2") names how many there are ALTOGETHER, not just the second one; one "2" stands for the whole group of two."
- **stage** — symbolize
- **teaching action(s)** — `reveal` (the consolidation/move of two per-object tags → one group "2" IS the teaching; the picture delivers it) → `spaced-recall` (light end-recall: re-voice the total naming the whole at the final hold — this single beat doubles as the lesson's one retrieval recap and its §6 reserved pulse).
- **narration beat (INTENT, no copy)** — prepare the consolidation aloud WITHOUT pre-saying the answer (§4 leakage rule): the narration sets up the question of how many altogether, then stops so the picture can deliver the truth that the one "2" stands for the whole group; only AFTER the picture reveals does the narration re-voice the total naming the whole (the recall pulse — 二 naming the whole group of two). The consolidation/move of the two per-object tags into one group "2" carries the discovery; apples stay quiet.
- **required visual** — the two per-object tags (1, 2) consolidating into a single group "2" that represents the total; the consolidation/move IS the focal change that carries the discovery (narration must not pre-say it). Read OFF `reveal.requires`: the focal change carries the discovery; `spaced-recall.requires`: the visual punctuation lands on the currently-spoken total. **Gap flag (named demand for W3b):** a tag→cardinal consolidation capability — "the per-object ordinal tags (1, 2) move/merge into a single group-total glyph ("2") representing the whole set, with the apples holding quiet." Not assumed present; W3b resolves against the live catalog.

## Preceding beat — topic-intro

Every lesson opens with `announce-topic`. It precedes the two teaching cues (it is NOT one of the two discoveries — it names what we'll learn, it carries no discovery of its own).

### announce-topic
- **teaching action(s)** — `announce-topic`.
- **narration beat (INTENT, no copy)** — name the lesson: we will learn to count to two and find out how many. Read alone first, THEN the teaching objects (the apples) enter after it has read.
- **required visual** — `LessonIntroCard`; while the title/teaser reads it is the ONLY readable thing on screen — cast/teaching objects enter AFTER the title has read, never overlaid on it (binding beat-ordering note for the composer: title-read → apple-entry, in sequence, never overlaid).

## Reinforcement cues (and reasoning)

- **None as separate cues.** Pedagogy is insight-shaped (§8), one clean delivery per discovery: cue-1 has no replay (tagging-in-turn IS the model); cue-2 has ONE end-recall (re-voiced total) which is also the lesson's only retrieval recap and §6 reserved pulse. Because cue-2's end-recall re-voices the count-and-total together, pedagogy explicitly routes the recap role INTO cue-2 — so there is no second closing retrieval cue. This obeys the skill's "ship exactly ONE closing retrieval recap" rule.
