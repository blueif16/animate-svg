# kptest-compare-more-fewer

**Audience.** 3–5 years old, Mandarin Chinese narration.
**Length.** 75–105 seconds — HINT for scope, not a target the composer pads to.
**Builds on.** none
**Style.** default

## Knowledge point
The child leaves knowing how to compare two small groups (≤5) by quantity: one group has MORE (多), the other has FEWER (少), and "more" and "fewer" are two directions of the SAME comparison — 五比三多 describes the same picture as 三比五少. Which group has more is decided by one-to-one matching, not by how spread out or how big the objects look.

## The one beat
Two rows of identical dots sit one above the other — five red dots on top, three blue dots below — and each top dot drops a line straight down to pair with one bottom dot. The two top dots left WITHOUT a partner are the whole point: the child sees that five has two left over, so 五比三多 (five is more than three); and the very same paired picture, read the other way, is 三比五少 (three is fewer than five).

## Out of scope
No numerals-as-written-symbols lesson, no addition/subtraction, no "how many more" counting of the gap (a later KP). Only the MORE/FEWER comparison of two given groups, each ≤5.

## Continuity
Reuse the counting-dot primitive and a one-to-one pairing layout. Identity-invariant: a dot stays the same dot before and after the rows align for matching.

## Narration notes
A 比 comparison must name BOTH groups and the direction in one complete utterance (五比三多 / 三比五少), never a stranded "比三多" with the subject dropped. Teach both directions. Mandarin number words; calm, spaced early-math pacing with wait-time after each comparison.
