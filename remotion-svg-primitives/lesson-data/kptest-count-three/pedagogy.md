# Pedagogy — kptest-count-three

lesson kind: math-insight   (counting-skill acquisition leading to one cardinality insight; §9 carve-out does NOT apply — this is not an L2 lesson)

---

## Cue 1 — First apple, first number

discovery:     One object gets exactly one number word. This apple is "one" (一).
stage:         concrete → represent (the real apple appears; the numeral tag "1" is a representational overlay the child learns to read alongside the concrete object)
focal:         the first apple and its "1" tag — the apple's arrival followed by the tag attaching is the visual change that delivers "one thing, one number"
reinforcement: minimal on its own — this is the first of three counting beats that together build the counting rhythm; the single exposure here is one step in a three-step acquisition sequence

## Cue 2 — Second apple, count grows

discovery:     The counting sequence grows by exactly one with each new object. After "one" comes "two" (二) — and this second apple is the "two."
stage:         represent (the child now reads both the apple and its tag together as a counting unit; the "2" tag on the second apple is the representation of "how far we've counted")
focal:         the second apple arriving and its "2" tag attaching — everything else (the first apple and its "1" tag) is stable and quiet, so the child's eye goes to the new arrival
reinforcement: this is the second application of the same counting rule — the child begins to form the expectation "each new thing gets the next number"; the repetition from cue 1 is the acquisition mechanism (practice, not decoration)

## Cue 3 — Third apple, pattern confirmed

discovery:     The rule holds again: a third object gets the third number word (三). By now the child can anticipate the count before the tag appears.
stage:         represent (same representational reading as cue 2; the child should now be comfortable with the apple-tag pairing as a unit)
focal:         the third apple arriving and its "3" tag attaching — the counting rhythm is now a three-beat pattern the child has experienced, not just heard about
reinforcement: this is the third and final counting beat; the three-beat rhythm (一, 二, 三) is the natural acquisition loop for "counting to three" — if the child can anticipate "三" before the narrator says it, the counting skill has landed; this third repetition is what separates a pattern from a coincidence for a 3–5 year old

## Cue 4 — Cardinality: the last number names the whole group

discovery:     The last number we said — "three" — is not just a label for the third apple. It names how many apples there are altogether.
stage:         symbolize (the solitary large "3" is a pure symbol standing for the entire group's quantity, abstracted away from any single apple or its position in the counting sequence)
focal:         the transition from per-apple tags to a single prominent "3" that represents the group total — this visual change (individual labels recede or are replaced by one group-level symbol) is what delivers the cardinality insight
reinforcement: insight beat — one clean delivery plus a hold for the child to absorb; no replay needed because the three preceding counting beats already primed the child to be thinking about "three"; the cardinality moment lands as the *answer* to a question the counting rhythm implicitly posed ("and what does three mean?"); the hold after delivery gives processing time

---

## Pedagogical reasoning (§8 — rhythm and reinforcement)

Counting to three for a 3–5 year old is an **acquisition task** (a skill to practice), not a single insight. The three counting cues (1–3) are the practice loop: each one applies the same rule ("new object → next number") to a new instance, building the expectation through repetition. This is proportional reinforcement — three exposures for a brand-new skill the child has never performed deliberately.

The cardinality cue (4) is an **insight task** — the child either sees that "3" now means the whole group, or they don't. One clean delivery with a hold is the right dose. Replay would not help an insight the way it helps a word or a sound.

No setup beat is needed: there is no prior counting state to contrast against (the brief builds on nothing), and no child needs convincing that "counting is hard" before they'll appreciate counting. The lesson starts with the first apple.

The total lesson should land around 20–25 seconds — the brief's ~25s hint is appropriate for four cues at this pace. The length emerges from the three-beat counting rhythm (which cannot be compressed below three beats) plus one insight beat with a hold.

## Narration-leakage guard (§4)

The narrator must NOT count ahead of the visual. The correct division of labor:

- **Bad:** narrator says "一个、两个、三个" in one breath while apples appear. The child hears the count without doing any visual counting work.
- **Good:** narrator says "一个" as the first apple lands and its tag attaches. Then "再来一个…两个" as the second arrives. Then "还有一个…三个" as the third arrives. The narration *names the action* ("再来一个" = one more arrives); the visual *delivers the count* (the child sees the apple and watches the tag attach).
- **Cardinality beat:** narrator may say "数完了，一共三个" (we finished counting — three altogether). Naming the total is acceptable here because the insight is *what "three" means*, not *what the number is* — the child already knows the count is three from the visual tags.

## Language note

This is a Mandarin-medium counting lesson, not an L2 lesson. The §9 carve-out does not apply. Narration is in Mandarin throughout. The target utterances (一, 二, 三) are the child's L1 number words, not foreign sounds to acquire.
