# kp2-counting-by-tens — pedagogy

Walking in, the child has just finished KP1: they know that ten loose ones can be bundled into one ten, and they recognize the bundle as an object. They have *not* yet used the bundle as a *counting unit* — they have not yet experienced the act of counting tens the way they count ones, and they have not yet felt the speed difference. This lesson's job is to move them from "the bundle exists" to "the bundle is a thing I count with."

The arc opens by letting the child *feel* the cost of counting by ones (Beat 1), then collapses that same ten into one named count (Beat 2), then extends that single count into a sequence so the operation becomes a pattern (Beats 3–4), and closes by making the speed difference visible side-by-side (Beat 5).

---

## Beat 1 — Loose count, felt

discovery:  the child notices that counting ten loose ones takes ten separate counts — that reaching "ten" by ones is a long walk, not a single step.
stage:      represent
focal:      the running count advancing one tick at a time across the ten loose sticks (the count sequence itself is what changes — the sticks are static)

## Beat 2 — The bundle is one count

discovery:  the child notices that the *same* ten sticks, once bundled, are named and counted as a single thing — "一个十" — so one bundle equals one count of a new unit.
stage:      represent
focal:      the bundled ten arriving as a single counted item with the count "1 / 一个十" attaching to it (the count display going from "ten steps" to "one step" is what changes)

## Beat 3 — Tens count like ones

discovery:  the child notices that counting works on tens the same way it worked on ones — after "一个十" comes "两个十," exactly as after "一" came "二."
stage:      represent
focal:      the second bundle entering and the tens-count advancing 1 → 2 (the advance of the count is the discovery; the bundle itself is the carrier)

## Beat 4 — The pattern holds

discovery:  the child notices that each new bundle adds exactly one to the tens-count — the rule from Beat 3 wasn't a one-off, it generalizes.
stage:      represent
focal:      the third bundle entering and the tens-count advancing 2 → 3 (the third instance is what turns "maybe a pattern" into "yes, a pattern")

## Beat 5 — Why this is the faster way

discovery:  the child notices that counting the same large pile by tens reaches the total in far fewer counts than counting it by ones — the new unit is the convenient unit when there are many things.
stage:      represent
focal:      the side-by-side contrast between the long row of loose ones and the short row of bundles, with their respective counts visible at the same time (the comparison of count-lengths is what changes when both come into view together)
