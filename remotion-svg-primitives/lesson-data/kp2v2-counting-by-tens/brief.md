# kp2v2-counting-by-tens

**Audience.** Age 6–7, first grade. Mandarin Chinese narration.
**Length.** 35–55 seconds.
**Builds on.** `ten-ones-make-one-ten` (child has just learned 10 ones = 1 ten).
**Style.** ink-wash

## Knowledge point
"一" and "十" are both counting units; "十" is the convenient unit when there are many things. The child should leave knowing: counting by tens is faster than counting by ones, because each "1个十" already holds 10 ones.

## The one beat
Same 10 sticks, counted two ways. Loose: 一、二、三…十 — ten counts. Bundled: "1个十" — one count. Then extend: 2 捆 = "2个十", 3 捆 = "3个十". Each new bundle adds 1 to the tens count but holds 10 ones inside. The speedup is visible at a glance.

## Out of scope
Do not name the numbers 20, 30, etc. (KP3/4). Do not show the written digit "10" or use 十位/个位 (KP3). Do not say "计数单位" out loud — use "一" / "十" as units demonstrated by example. Do not re-teach the bundling action — KP1 already did that; here the bundle is a given.

## Continuity
Reuse `SmallStick`, `StickGroup`, `BundleWrap`, `StepTally`, `LabelCallout`, `CountStepIndicator`, `TeacherMark`, `Sketch`. Same cream background, orange sticks, coral rope, navy ink as KP1. Identity-invariant: every stick is the same orange `SmallStick` across KP1 and KP2; a bundle in KP2 looks exactly like a KP1 bundle. New bundles slide in atomically — never re-animate 10 sticks bundling again.

## Narration notes
Slow voice plus short script lands ~35–55s at ~8 cues. If Gemini stutters on enumerated 一、二、三…十 (KP1 precedent on 一一 repetition), prefer framing narration ("我们一根一根地数") over literal enumeration.
