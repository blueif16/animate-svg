# kptest-teen-numbers

**Audience.** 一年级 (~6–7 years old), Mandarin-speaking children. Narration in Chinese.
**Length.** 60–90s band — HINT for scope only, not a target to pad to.
**Builds on.** kp3-tens-and-ones-place (tens and ones) and ten-ones-make-one-ten.
**Style.** default

## Knowledge point
The child leaves knowing a **teen number is "一个十 and some 一"** — 11 is 一个十 和 一个一 (十一), 12 is 一个十 和 两个一 (十二), … through 十九. The bundle-of-ten plus the loose ones IS the number.

## The one beat
One bundle of ten (tied) sits on the left; loose ones drop in on the right — one loose one → "十一", two → "十二". The child watches the loose ones count up beside the fixed ten and hears the name form: 十 + 几 → 十几.

## Out of scope
No place-value digit notation (the "1" in the tens column, "1X"). No counting past 19, no 二十. No addition symbols. The discovery is the STRUCTURE (ten + ones), named in Chinese number words, not written digits.

## Continuity
Reuse the registered bundle / loose-stick primitives from the place-value line — the ten-bundle is identity-invariant (never untied); only the loose ones change count.

## Narration notes
Chinese only. NAME the action ("再放一根") and let the picture deliver the count and the name. Insight + light practice: model 十一/十二 slowly, then let the child predict 十三…十九 in a quick spaced recall. Do not pad.
