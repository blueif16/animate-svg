# kptest-fenyuhe-five

**Audience.** 一年级 (~6–7 years old), Mandarin-speaking children. Narration in Chinese.
**Length.** 60–90s band — HINT for scope only, not a target to pad to.
**Builds on.** kp1-fen-yu-he-intro (the child knows 分 means split, 合 means combine).
**Style.** default

## Knowledge point
The child leaves able to do **5的分与合** — 5 can be split (分) into 1和4 and 2和3 (and the symmetric 4和1, 3和2), and the parts combine (合) back into 5. The part–part–whole relation for the number 5.

## The one beat
Five dots in one group; they split into two groups — 1和4 — then slide back together into 5 (合). Then split a different way — 2和3 — and recombine. The child sees the SAME five become different part-pairs and合 back, feeling that the whole is conserved across the split.

## Out of scope
No other numbers' decompositions (6, 7… are later sections). No addition/subtraction symbols (+, −, =). No counting past five. ONLY 5的分与合.

## Continuity
Reuse the registered dot / part–whole primitives (e.g. the conservation-morph / part-whole composer) — the five dots are identity-invariant; only the grouping changes. The SAME dots 分 and 合, never destroyed and recreated.

## Narration notes
Chinese only. NAME the action (分 / 合) and let the picture deliver which parts result. These bonds are an acquisition target — model 1和4 and 2和3, then a learner-response beat ("你来分分看：5可以分成几和几?") with a real wait-time gap before the reveal. Spaced-recall recap of both splits. Each split is a co-equal beat; don't over-weight one.
