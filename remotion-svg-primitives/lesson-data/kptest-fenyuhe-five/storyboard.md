# kptest-fenyuhe-five Storyboard

This lesson teaches the part–part–whole relationship for the number 5: five can be split into 1和4 or 2和3, and the parts combine back into 5. The child discovers conservation of quantity and practices recalling the splits.

### cue-announce-topic-split-1of4
- discovery ref: The child discovers that five can be split into one and four.
- stage: concrete
- teaching action(s): announce-topic → model-target-slow → reveal
- narration beat intent: announce the lesson topic, then model the bond "一和四", then the picture delivers the insight that five splits into one and four.
- required visual: 
  - announce-topic: title "5的分与合" reads alone, no other readable content.
  - model-target-slow: bond glyph "一和四" big, centered, nothing on top, held for the spoken length.
  - reveal: five dots separating into one dot and four dots (the split).

### cue-conserve-1of4
- discovery ref: The child discovers that when one and four are combined, the total is still five (conservation).
- stage: concrete
- teaching action(s): model-target-slow → reveal
- narration beat intent: model the action "合", then the picture delivers the insight that combining one and four gives five.
- required visual:
  - model-target-slow: glyph "合" big, centered, nothing on top, held for the spoken length.
  - reveal: the group of one dot and the group of four dots moving together to form one group of five.

### cue-split-2of3
- discovery ref: The child discovers that five can also be split into two and three.
- stage: concrete

- stage: concrete
- teaching action(s): model-target-slow → reveal
- narration beat intent: model the action "合", then the picture delivers the insight that combining two and three gives five.

  - model-target-slow: glyph "合" big, centered, nothing on top, held for the spoken length.

### cue-learner-response-gap
- discovery ref: The child discovers that they can recall how to split five into two parts (they actively retrieve the splits from memory).
- stage: concrete
- teaching action(s): invite-echo → learner-response-gap
- narration beat intent: invite the child to recall how to split five, then hold silence for them to think.
- required visual:
  - invite-echo: a clear "your turn" cue (e.g., a prompt icon or text inviting response).
  - learner-response-gap: hold the visual of the question (or a neutral "thinking" prompt) through the silent gap; no narration audio.

### cue-reveal-answer
- discovery ref: The child discovers that one correct split is one and four (or two and three) — the answer to the question.
- stage: concrete
- teaching action(s): reveal
- narration beat intent: the picture delivers the insight that one correct split (e.g., 一和四) is the answer to the question.
- required visual: the appearance of the one-and-four split (or the highlighting of that split) as the revealed answer.

### cue-spaced-recap-both
- discovery ref: The child discovers that both splits (1和4 and 2和3) are valid ways to split five, reinforcing the relations.
- stage: concrete
- teaching action(s): spaced-recall
- narration beat intent: recall both splits in rapid succession.
- required visual: the rapid display of both splits in sequence (or side-by-side), highlighting each bond as it is shown.
