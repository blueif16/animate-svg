# kp1-hello-greetings — Pedagogy Gate (Wave 0)

The lesson teaches three **spoken English routines** as usable social moves, not vocabulary
to memorize. The "unit" being learned is a *routine* (a thing you say at a moment): greet on
meeting, name yourself, part on leaving. The whole lesson is ONE tiny encounter between the
SAME two kids — greeting → self-intro → farewell — so the child discovers that these phrases
belong to *moments in a real meeting*, not to a flashcard list.

**Cognitive-ladder note (skill §3) for a language lesson.** The three stages map to:
- **concrete** — the child watches a routine *performed in its real moment* (a kid waves and
  says it at the gate). The "object" being operated on is the social action.
- **represent** — the spoken form is surfaced as a read-along highlight the child can track and
  say along with; the sound is made trackable without spelling it.
- **symbolize** — would be letters/spelling/IPA on screen. This lesson NEVER reaches stage 3:
  alphabet is §1.3 and is explicitly out of scope. No cue may show a stage above `represent`.

**Match-the-spoken-count rule (skill §7), language form.** When a phrase is said aloud, the
on-screen read-along highlight must surface *that exact phrase* and no other — the moving
highlight is the "count display" for sound. The I'm beat especially: highlight `I'm` as ONE
swelling unit (one syllable /aɪm/), never split into two ticks.

**Narration discipline (skill §4).** Teacher narration is Chinese ("看，他在打招呼" / "该说再见啦"):
it *names the moment* (meeting / introducing / parting). The English target word is *delivered by
the character in the picture*, on the wave, in the bubble — the picture (the kid speaking)
delivers the routine; the Chinese narration only frames the moment. If the Chinese narration
spoke the English answer, the picture would be decoration.

cue: intro
discovery:  The child learns this video's job — today we learn to say hello, say who we are, and say goodbye in English — and meets the two kids who will carry every phrase.
stage:      represent
focal:      the topic-intro title card (lesson title + Unit 1 "Hello!" teaser) resolving in; the two kid faces appear quiet beneath it as the cast for what follows.

cue: meet-hello
discovery:  When you meet someone, the English greeting is "Hello!" / "Hi!" — and the child sees it is the thing you say AT the moment of meeting (one kid waves at the gate, the greeting rides the wave).
stage:      concrete
focal:      the left kid's wave + the "Hello!" speech bubble popping in on the wave (DialogueExchange turn 1, gesture:'wave'); the read-along surfaces "Hello!" / "Hi!" as it lands.

cue: intro-self
discovery:  After greeting, you tell someone who you are with "I'm ___" — and the child discovers the precise SOUND of "I'm": one syllable /aɪm/, modeled BIG and slow, not [em] and not two beats [ai-m].
stage:      represent
focal:      the right kid's reply bubble "Hi! I'm Sam." with the emphasis flag firing ONE pulse on "I'm" (DialogueExchange turn 2, emphasis:true); ReadAlongHighlight swells "I'm" as a single held unit so the child hears and tracks it as one beat.

cue: part-goodbye
discovery:  When you leave someone, the English farewell is "Goodbye!" / "Bye-Bye!" — said AT the moment of parting (the same two kids wave as they walk apart), closing the encounter that opened with Hello.
stage:      concrete
focal:      both kids' parting wave + the "Goodbye!" / "Bye-Bye!" bubbles (DialogueExchange farewell turns, gesture:'wave'); the read-along surfaces the farewell phrase as it is spoken.

cue: recap
discovery:  The child sees the whole social arc as one connected sequence — meeting (Hello) → naming (I'm…) → parting (Goodbye) — confirming the three phrases are three moments of ONE encounter, not three separate words to drill.
stage:      represent
focal:      the three phrases lined up as the encounter's beginning-middle-end (recap punctuation pulse — the lesson's second and final allowed pulse, per taste's two-pulse rule, paired with the intro-self emphasis pulse); the two kids stay present so the arc reads as theirs.
