## Scope rule (user directive, 2026-06-18) — what Hermes optimizes here
Hermes stewards the **textual quality of the skills / markdowns / registry-item prose that push our flow** — we debug and optimize that content. A fix may land in **markdown OR a component code-snippet** (a primitive's `.tsx` is fair game when the flaw is the component itself). What is **OUT of Hermes edit-scope** (tracked as a system's *wiring*, not optimized here, at least for now) is the **orchestration / executor code**: `.claude/workflows/lesson-build.js`, `.claude/workflows/capability-gap-filler.js`, and the `pi-runner/` executor. Improve the chain's *behavior* by editing the SKILL a node reads, not the plumbing.

## How the four relate (the routing map)
The three capability systems are the reusable library; **lesson-build consumes it**. So a component-quality flaw surfaced inside a lesson routes to a capability system, not to lesson-build:
- **lesson-build** *consumes* the library (W2a/W3b/W4a read the catalog-digest + browse the gallery to pick components).
- **capability-registry** *gates* it (the drift-clean source of truth + the item prose agents cite).
- **capability-gallery** *exposes & audits* it (the human/agent surface to browse + visually judge quality).
- **capability-gap-filler** *grows* it (detect gaps → author new registered capabilities).

**Cross-cutting threads** (one that genuinely spans two systems) get **one owner + a "touches: `<id>`" pointer** — logged once, never in both rows.
