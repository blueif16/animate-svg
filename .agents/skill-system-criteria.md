# Skill-system output criteria — animation-test

_The per-node **OUTPUT ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA** fixture — the standing, human-judged quality bar for each producing node's artifact. Sibling of `.agents/skill-system-map.md` (composition / responsibilities / diagnostics) and of the `contract()` Output Contract in `lesson-build.js` (mechanical existence + lane). First generated by the `node-output-criteria` workflow; refined by hand whenever our expectation of a node's output changes (a richer shape, more data from a node, a new must-have)._

**What this is FOR.** (1) the standard the human's eye judges every future run against, so we converge on quality run-over-run; (2) the improvement target we sharpen as the system matures (alongside the map's Diagnostics log).

**What this is NOT.** It is a **judging fixture, never a node input** — NEVER inject these criteria into a producing node's prompt. Doing so makes the executor teach-to-the-test (it optimizes to the rubric, not the skill) and voids the clean-room signal that tells us whether the SKILL ITSELF — not the rubric — produces good output (see `hermes-skill-system` law 3 + the workflow's READING LAW). The criteria are read **here**, by the steward, to judge a run and to aim the next skill edit; they never travel into the run being judged. They are also NOT auto-graded/reward-hackable tests — expectations a human checks.

**Coverage.** 12 producing nodes. Setup (trivial scaffold) and W6 verification (retired) are omitted. Each entry: the artifact(s) → its downstream purpose → acceptance criteria (must-haves + quality dimensions) → red flags (what bad looks like). Node ids match the map's wave DAG.

**Per-node exemplars (2026-06-11).** Each node carries a 🟢 **Gold** + 🔴 **Red-flag** — REAL artifact excerpts (copied verbatim, never invented), the calibration anchor the eye diffs a new run against (judge by *difference from the gold*, not cold-reading). Status: **VALIDATED** (confirmed by a clean-room rerun + judge), **PROVISIONAL** (best real artifact, not yet rerun-validated), or **PENDING** (no real artifact yet — slot reserved). When a fresh run would flip a frozen verdict, the criterion has been *redefined* — route that through the Hermes human gate, never a silent edit.

## W0 pedagogy — `lesson-data/<id>/pedagogy.md`

**Purpose.** The teaching contract every downstream wave reads: per cue, the single thing the child discovers + its cognitive stage + focal element + reasoned reinforcement, so storyboard/visual/audio/composer build to ONE discovery per beat and never the wrong teaching object.

**Acceptance criteria**
- Header declares lesson kind (math-insight | language/L2 | acquisition | …) and the declaration is honest about the §4 carve-out it triggers — an acquisition/L2 kind means the voice is EXPECTED to name the target fully; a math-insight kind means the picture must reveal it and the voice must withhold it. The kind chosen matches the brief's actual content.
- Every cue paragraph carries all four required lines — discovery / stage / focal / reinforcement — in that order, and each discovery is ONE sentence stating what the child knows leaving that they did NOT know walking in (not a restatement of the narration, not "show X").
- Each discovery fits exactly ONE of the four shapes (new unit | new operation | relation between known units | felt-property setup), and carries exactly one — a beat with two metaphors is split. Setup beats earn their place only by setting up a following beat's contrast; a setup with no payoff, or one whose felt property is already obvious, is dropped.
- Each cue's `stage` (concrete | represent | symbolize) never exceeds the lesson's declared ceiling, and the out-of-scope boundary from the brief is honored at the stage level (e.g. a no-symbols lesson keeps every beat at concrete/represent, not just "no digit happens to appear").
- The `focal` names the single element that CHANGES during the beat (a motion, a count advancing, a state flipping, a label attaching) — never a static tableau; one focal per cue, everything else implicitly quiet.
- Reinforcement is REASONED per cue, not templated: it states how much encountering THIS discovery needs (proportional to its novelty/difficulty) and which move(s) — or honestly "none (insight, one pass)". Acquisition targets show the time-FLOOR discipline (multiple spaced exposures, a real learner-response wait-time gap, and a later spaced recall), and the plan reads as emergent from content, neither rushed to nor padded to the brief's length hint.
- When the knowledge point enumerates several co-equal routines/targets, a whole-set sanity check is visible: each sibling gets its own staged delivery AND a retrieval in the recap, airtime stays roughly balanced, and the hardest item gets SOMEWHAT more practice ON TOP of the others' baseline — never several dedicated cues while a co-equal sibling is compressed to one.
- A spaced-recall / retrieval beat brings the targets back later so they are retrieved, not just re-shown, and (for relational/part–whole content) every relation or retrieval beat is planned as a COMPLETE natural utterance that names the whole, not a token.

**Red flags**
- A backwards/fragmentary relation or retrieval beat that drops the whole being decomposed or suppresses the conserved total — a split spoken as a stranded token ("one and five", "split into", or a bare recap list "one-five, two-four, three-three") instead of the full utterance a teacher says ("six splits into one and five", "one and five make six"). A relation spoken without its whole is not the relation.
- A cue whose honest discovery is "the spoken sentence" — narration masquerading as teaching (filler); or a cue with an empty/fuzzy discovery line that fits none of the four shapes.
- Teaching what is merely mathematically/logically true instead of what THIS child is learning to do (a unit-cost badge, a notation chip, an adjacent-operation caption) — smuggling the teacher's why or a later KP into the current beat.
- A beat operating above the lesson's reached stage — introducing a symbol/digit/equation before the concrete and represent stages have been earned, even as a "fun fact".
- Narration leakage for an INSIGHT or a count the child should perform: the voice announces the count or names the aha before the picture delivers it (distinct from the legitimate acquisition carve-out, where naming the target fully IS the delivery — confusing the two in either direction is a red flag).
- One-pass slideshow pacing on acquisition content: a single clean exposure per item, crammed repeats counted as separate exposures, no real wait-time silence, no spaced recall.
- Breadth starvation: the hard/highlighted item gets several dedicated cues while a co-equal sibling routine is name-checked or compressed to one short cue.
- A focal that is a static end-frame the child reads rather than a change the child watches; or multiple cognitive tasks bundled into one cue (count AND name, transform AND read-the-result-label) that should be split.
- Reinforcement written as a copied template identical across cues regardless of each discovery's difficulty.

**Exemplars**
- 🟢 **Gold** — `lesson-data/kptest-fenyuhe-six/pedagogy.md` · VALIDATED `a3b38fe`
  > lesson kind: **math-acquisition** (the three splits + their 合 are facts the child must produce on demand, not an insight the picture reveals)
  > The complete utterances "6可以分成1和5" / "1和5合成6" name the whole being decomposed — never a fragment like "一和五" spoken without its whole.

  _Why gold:_ honest acquisition lesson-kind triggers the §4 carve-out; every relation/retrieval beat is a complete utterance naming the whole + conserved total.
- 🔴 **Red-flag** — `_prior-runs/kptest-fenyuhe-six/pedagogy.PRE-FIX.md` · surfaced by `a3b38fe`
  > lesson kind: math-insight
  > The §4 narration-leakage rule applies normally — the picture delivers the split, the narration names the result.

  _Why red:_ mislabels an acquisition lesson as math-insight and forces the voice to withhold/fragment the bond → the downstream backwards fragment "一和五，分成。" that drops the whole 六 and suppresses conservation.

## W1 storyboard — `lesson-data/<id>/storyboard.md`

**Purpose.** The cue spine — the ordered, ID'd list of cues (per-cue discovery ref, teaching action(s), narration-beat intent, required visual, and an `exposures` ledger) that every downstream wave binds to for voice, visuals, captions, and timing.

**Acceptance criteria**
- Cue-spine completeness: a short lesson-shape paragraph plus one `### <cue-id>` block per cue in play order; each block carries ALL five required fields — discovery ref (verbatim from pedagogy.md), stage, teaching action(s), narration-beat intent, required visual. A missing field on any cue makes the artifact unusable downstream.
- Pedagogy fidelity: every cue's discovery ref is a verbatim sentence from pedagogy.md (one cue ⇒ one discovery), no discovery is invented, merged, or silently dropped, and no cue's stage exceeds the stage pedagogy assigned it. The storyboard realizes pedagogy's rhythm; it does not re-decide the teaching.
- Teaching-action grounding: every cue is tagged with one or more moves from `.agents/TEACHING-ACTIONS.md` (chains allowed, e.g. model → gloss → invite-echo); no cue is filler. The teaching verb is decided before any layout.
- Required-visual is teaching-driven, not layout-improvised: each cue's required visual is read OFF its teaching action(s)' `requires` and names real component/primitive vocabulary; genuine gaps are flagged as NAMED demands for Wave 3, never invented components or drawn layouts. Move-level layout laws (e.g. announce-topic: title reads ALONE first, cast enters after) are carried as explicit beat-ordering notes.
- Reinforcement rhythm is a real SEQUENCE of cues, not one-cue-per-idea: distinct model / replay / practice / recall cues are allocated where pedagogy calls for it; replays are marked `replay of <cue-id>`; a cumulative spaced-recall recap is present; each invite-echo + wait-time gets its OWN cue so the silent learner-response beat is a real downstream window.
- Spine is BALANCED across co-equal routines/targets: each gets its own staged delivery cue plus recap retrieval, with extra practice cues added to the key-difficult target ON TOP of (not instead of) the others. A reader can tally cues per routine and confirm none is starved.
- Discipline boundaries respected: NO durations, NO frame numbers, NO code, and NO invented narration copy anywhere — narration fields state INTENT, not words or seconds. A syncable target word is placed at the HEAD of its cue.
- Accurate machine-readable `exposures` ledger at the end: per acquisition target, the count of spaced encounters that actually appear in the spine (model + choral + individual + recap), matching the real cues rather than aspirational counts — the reconcile reads this for the comprehension-floor advisory.

**Red flags**
- Narrated-slideshow spine: one cue per idea, no reinforcement/replay/recap cues — covers the content but teaches none of it and will run absurdly short.
- Propagating an upstream narration-intent fragment unchallenged so the spine ships with two near-identical closing beats (e.g. an aggregator/answer-reveal cue and a recap cue that say effectively the same thing) instead of one retrieval recap that re-practices all targets together.
- Unbalanced spine: several cues lavished on the hardest target while a co-equal routine is squeezed into one short cue or merely mentioned.
- Frame numbers, second/duration estimates, actual narration wording, or code appearing in any cue block.
- Layout-driven required visuals: jumping from discovery straight to a drawn scene with no teaching action in the middle, or inventing/building components instead of naming existing vocabulary and flagging real gaps as demands.
- Discovery refs paraphrased or fabricated rather than quoted verbatim; a cue serving two discoveries; or a pedagogy discovery dropped/merged with no trace.
- invite-echo and its wait-time buried inside a model cue (no standalone echo cue), so the ≥3–5s silent learner-response beat cannot become real silence downstream.
- Exposures ledger missing, or counts that are aspirational / don't match the cues actually present in the spine.

**Exemplars**
- 🟢 **Gold** — `lesson-data/kptest-fenyuhe-six/storyboard.md` · VALIDATED `893e490`+`c089d71`
  > narration beat intent: the teacher voices the COMPLETE bond, in its own breath-group — "6可以分成1和5" on the split, "1和5合成6" on the recombine. The voice names the whole being decomposed; the picture delivers the parts.

  _Why gold:_ bond intent stated referentially as a complete utterance (both directions); the two closers are kept only because their retrieval functions are explicitly distinct.
- 🔴 **Red-flag** — `_prior-runs/kptest-fenyuhe-six/storyboard.PRE-W1FIX.md` · surfaced by `893e490`
  > narration beat intent: announce the lesson topic "6的分与合", then model the bond "一和五", then the picture delivers the insight that six splits into one and five.
  > ### cue-reveal-answer … ### cue-spaced-recap-all-three … recall all three splits in rapid succession — 1和5, then 2和4, then 3和3

  _Why red:_ stranded-token bond intent ("model the bond 一和五" — the whole 六 dangles) + two near-identical closing beats (reveal-answer + recap) instead of one retrieval recap — red-flag 56.

## W2a visual-design — `lesson-data/<id>/visual-design.md`

**Purpose.** The Visual Contract every downstream wave reads: fixes the metaphor, the disjoint legibility zones, the identity-invariant teaching object, and the per-cue visualMotionSeconds budget that W2b narration is written to and W3.5 reconcile uses as the visual side of max(narration, motion).

**Acceptance criteria**
- Follows the mandated order of operations: a kids-eye §1 measurement block (composition, short-side, teaching-unit name + min/target in px, label/caption minimums) FIRST, then palette + motion vocabulary, then the §1 Visual Contract, then per-cue choreography intent — not jumped straight to per-cue layout.
- Declares named spatial zones that are GENUINELY disjoint — every zone's stated bounding box (x,y,w,h) is non-overlapping with the others, OR any intentional overlap is resolved by explicit time-sequencing/z-order so no readable content (title, label, caption, prompt, teaching glyph) is ever occluded. Every scene element is assigned to a named zone; nothing positions itself by "near the object".
- States ONE visual metaphor for the whole lesson and an identity-invariant: the teaching object is the same primitive at the same tone/shape/size across every transformation, with only count/layout/scale/opacity allowed to change (no hue shift, no shape swap, no "before in gray / after in color").
- Provides a per-cue visualMotionSeconds budget for EVERY cue, given as an honest minimum that INCLUDES dwell/read time (not just transition duration), explicitly framed as intent feeding W3.5 reconcile = max(narration, motion) + tail — never absolute frames.
- Respects the quantified legibility floors from kids-eye §1: the teaching unit meets its px minimum/target at 1920×1080, primary labels ≥48px, body labels ≥36px, captions ≥56px, and the densest cue is sanity-checked to actually fit inside the frame within the declared zones (a real px arithmetic check, e.g. N units × size + gaps vs short-side).
- Spends the canvas as a budget, not a stage: states which axis is binding and shows the teaching content occupies a substantial share (~≥50%) of the non-binding axis at its fullest cue — the teaching object is the largest element, not a small figure floating in empty frame.
- Enforces element economy and color-as-semantic-channel: a text-budget line where every on-screen string is justified as load-bearing (redundant ones dropped), at most 2 stacked surfaces, ≤4 meaningful colors + background each with a stated meaning, and emphasis (sparkle/pulse/bouncy) reserved for ONE climax moment.
- Specifies primitives by REUSE/compose-first (named registered primitives with props), references the relevant pedagogy discovery per cue, and lists concrete refuse-on-sight anti-patterns — concrete and directly consumable, not fuzzy ("a friendly layout").

**Red flags**
- Zones declared "disjoint" whose own stated coordinates actually overlap (e.g. a prompt/label zone sitting inside the teaching-object zone) — a paper claim contradicted by the numbers; the single worst known failure.
- No quantified split/separation legibility floor and no fit-check: the dense cue is never verified to fit at the stated minimum sizes, so sub-minimum rendering is rationalized as "readable enough".
- Minimalist composition that leaves most of the frame empty — the teaching object floats in a thin band with no occupancy audit on the non-binding axis.
- Order skipped: per-cue choreography written before (or without) the kids-eye measurement block and the §1 Visual Contract — the root cause of label-overlaps-art and broken-identity bugs.
- Identity broken across the transformation: before/after drawn as different shapes/colors, or a "two pictures stapled together" comparison.
- visualMotionSeconds missing for some cues, given as absolute frames, or set to pure transition time with no dwell.
- Decoration creep: a card/panel/chrome behind the object that already has its own silhouette, a fifth meaningful color, the same fact shown twice (label + caption + badge), or emphasis effects on every cue diluting the climax.
- Vague, non-load-bearing contract lines ("a clean friendly layout", "shows the change"), or freestyled one-off SVG instead of naming reusable registered primitives.

**Exemplars**
- 🟢 **Gold** — `_prior-runs/kptest-fenyuhe-six/visual-design.VALIDATED-99be61f.md` · VALIDATED `99be61f`
  > **Disjointness (pairwise intersections, delivery frame):**
  > - `zone-objects` ∩ `zone-pointer` = `{}` (objects y=475–605; pointer y=350–450).
  > - `zone-objects` ∩ `zone-caption` = `{}` (objects y≤605; caption y≥920).

  _Why gold:_ "disjoint" is a COMPUTED claim — pairwise bbox intersections shown empty with the actual y-coordinates, plus a `separation-gap-min ≥ 6% short-side = 65px` floor and a densest-cue fit-check.
- 🔴 **Red-flag** — `_prior-runs/kptest-fenyuhe-six/visual-design.PRE-W2AFIX.md` · surfaced by `99be61f`
  > zone-objects:   x=120, y=400, w=1680, h=380    | the six dots. NOTHING ELSE.
  > zone-question:  x=240, y=440, w=1440, h=240    | (cue-learner-response-gap only)
  > Six dots at 130 px + 30 px gaps = 930 px ≈ 86% of 1080 — they fit

  _Why red:_ zones declared "Disjoint" but `zone-question` is fully contained in `zone-objects` (345600 px² overlap, both on-screen in the learner-gap cue, never computed); "86% of 1080" uses the wrong denominator for a horizontal 930px row (real 48% ÷1920) — "the single worst known failure."

## W2b audio/captions — `lesson-data/<id>/audio-captions.md` + `script-cues.json`

**Purpose.** The per-cue narration + caption text and the machine-readable CuePlan that drive Wave 3a voice/ASR generation and become the on-screen caption track.

**Acceptance criteria**
- Every narration string is grammatical, complete, and well-formed in its language: correct word order, the whole subject/object/result present, no truncated or fragmentary lines, and pedagogically faithful to that cue's discovery (a fluent human reads each line aloud and it makes sense as a teacher's spoken sentence).
- Beyond complete, every line is in **warm child-directed teacher register** (the `## Register` law): speaks TO the child (direct address / a look-hook, not a third-person statement at the content), hands the thinking over with at least one guiding question per teaching arc, states the reverse/insight in its own warm words (not a cloned mirror clause), VARIES every repeated beat (no single sentence-template stamped across cues), and uses natural spoken Mandarin particles — never flat 书面语. A complete-but-flat line is not finished.
- Exactly one row per cue in BOTH files and they agree: the cue IDs, narration text, and caption text in audio-captions.md match the corresponding fields in script-cues.json one-to-one (no cue in one file but missing in the other, no divergent wording).
- Each caption is the spoken narration verbatim (broken only at natural phrase boundaries when long) — never a paraphrase, never a separate authored sentence, never content the voice does not say.
- Narration honors the leakage rule: the narrator NAMES the action/unit/target and lets the picture deliver any count, relation, or reveal — except a genuine L2/utterance/acquisition target, which the narration MUST voice because hearing it is the teaching act (the §4 carve-out).
- Each cue's narration length is sized to its visualMotionSeconds budget per the calibrated voice rate (≈ within ±20%), AND acquisition/reinforcement cues carry their full move sequence (model → repeat → echo prompt → recap) sized to the floors — neither padded with filler nor starved to a terse minimum.
- Intra-cue and learner-response pauses are a typed `gap: { seconds, reason }` on the cue — NEVER an in-text ellipsis or padded/stretched text — and the gap's duration matches the pedagogy reinforcement intent (e.g. a real ≥3–5s learner-response wait).
- Each cue carries an ASR-risk note with a concrete mitigation or a documented accept decision: single-character/homophone hazards paired/broken/moved; a deliberate L2 target preserved (kept in narration, caption, phrase) and explicitly NOT flagged to strip.
- The report-back is present and self-consistent: total/per-cue char counts + estimated seconds vs each cue's visual budget, signed deltas, the ASR-risk flag list, and any leakage fixes made vs the storyboard draft.

**Red flags**
- Broken, backwards, or incomplete narration — fragments, dropped words, scrambled order, or a missing whole/result (e.g. an announce line stating the parts but omitting the number being split, or a conserve line omitting the recombined whole) — and captions that faithfully mirror the broken narration.
- **Robotic / stamped register** — the same sentence-template repeated across N cues (the canonical `<whole>可以分成<a>和<b>。<a>和<b>合成<whole>` ×3 stamp), 书面语 stiffness, a procedure announced at the content instead of direct address to the child, or an unnatural construction a real teacher would never say aloud (e.g. `我们来分六`). Complete but lifeless still fails.
- Caption diverges from spoken narration: paraphrased, summarized, translated, or carrying content the voice never says (or narration with no matching caption).
- script-cues.json and audio-captions.md disagree: different cue set, mismatched wording, or a cue in one and absent from the other; missing required fields (id/narration/phrase/caption).
- Leakage: the narration announces the count/answer/relation the visual is meant to reveal — or, conversely, an L2/sound/word acquisition target is Chinese-only'd / paraphrased away instead of being voiced.
- A pause written as an in-text ellipsis/dots (held-vowel drone) or faked by padding text, instead of a typed `gap`; or a learner-response/echo cue with no wait-time gap.
- Length abuse in either direction: padding to hit a brief target length, OR starving acquisition cues to one terse crammed line with too few exposures and no wait-time.
- ASR hazards left unaddressed and unflagged, or a deliberate L2 target wrongly listed as a hazard to delete.
- Frame numbers, absolute timings, or invented hold durations authored here, or hardcoded topic/answer copy assuming one specific lesson.

## W2c sound-design — `lesson-data/<id>/audio-cues.json` + `_logs/sound-design.md`

**Purpose.** The semantic sound manifest (bed mood, intro sting, outro resolve, SFX→beat map) that the W3c asset lane provisions and the W4 composer maps onto its own motion frames — declaring what the lesson sounds like, never when.

**Acceptance criteria**
- Schema-valid and complete: parses against AudioCues, carries the correct lessonId, exactly ONE bed key from the curated library, intro/outro/sfx fields present (intro.sting optional, outro.resolve defaulted on).
- Fully semantic — ZERO frames, offsets, or durations anywhere; every SFX row is `{cue, event, sound}` (+ optional perStep/risingPitch) keyed to a cue id, with the composer left to compute the frame.
- Bed mood matches the lesson kind: math/number → calm major bed, literacy → playful, pinyin/tone → the flat pad — a library key, not a bespoke per-lesson track description.
- `toneSafe` set correctly: true for any lesson where the child must hear a spoken lexical tone (pinyin/tone), false otherwise; when true, no melodic motif mapped under tone-teaching narration.
- SFX density restrained and motivated: ≤1 SFX per beat, every row corresponds to a real motion event documented in visual-design.md, at most ONE ta-da on the single success/discovery beat.
- The single ta-da/chime reward lands on the lesson's genuine pedagogical payoff beat identified in pedagogy.md, not an arbitrary/decorative moment.
- Each chosen SFX/bed/sting key resolves to the fixed sound vocabulary; any mood/asset genuinely absent is NAMED as a gap for W3c rather than silently invented.
- The companion `_logs/sound-design.md` justifies the bed + toneSafe choice, gives a one-line density rationale per sonified beat, names any asset gap, and flags any [ASSUMED] choice for Wave 6.

**Red flags**
- Any frame number, offset, ms/seconds duration, or absolute timing leaks into audio-cues.json.
- An SFX row with no motivating motion event, or density approaching one-per-frame / multiple sounds stacked on one beat.
- A reward sound (ta-da/chime) placed over teaching narration instead of a gap/after the line; or more than one ta-da.
- A tone/pinyin lesson left `toneSafe:false`, or a melodic bed/sting/pentatonic motif mapped under tone-teaching words.
- Bed mood mismatched (playful/melodic bed on a number lesson), or a bespoke per-lesson track invented instead of a curated key.
- An off-library asset used silently instead of named as a W3c gap, or new keys coined off-vocabulary.
- Hard-coded lesson specifics (topic strings, counts, Chinese narration) baked into the manifest as load-bearing.
- Missing/perfunctory `_logs/sound-design.md`; or treating placeholder-quality library assets as final without flagging the stand-in.

## W3a voice+ASR — `gemini-voice.json`, `asr-alignment.json`, `voice-clips.json`, per-cue clips, `<camel>Clips.ts`

**Purpose.** The frozen, ASR-verified per-cue voice clips + exact narrationFrames that lock the lesson's audio truth — every downstream wave reads this timing and never re-records.

**Acceptance criteria**
- EACH CUE'S SPOKEN WORDS MATCH THE SCRIPT, CUE BY CUE: no truncated, dropped, swapped, or extra words. Verified from the ACTUAL audio — per-clip ASR coverage of the expected phrase, plus a human spot-listen on any low-coverage clip. (The dedicated-TTS model emits NO `transcriptText` self-report, so coverage is read from ASR on the rendered WAV, never a model self-report.) This is the acquisition-quality bar the node protects — NOT the freeze gate (see next).
- TRUNCATION IS STRUCTURALLY PREVENTED, NOT GATED: the dedicated-TTS model (`gemini-3.1-flash-tts-preview`, single-payload `generateContent`) cannot cut mid-utterance, so full clips are the structural default; a per-cue truncation ADVISORY (per-clip ASR coverage + cohort-relative duration) is a NON-BLOCKING canary that surfaces residue for a spot-listen. The HARD freeze gate is drone / empty-short / dead-air only — a noisy ASR/cohort backstop must never block a clean render.
- PER-CUE ALIGNMENT CONFIDENCE IS HONEST AND HIGH: every cue carries a real matchScore/confidence; the reported status reflects the WORST cue, not the best. Low-evidence cues are re-rolled, re-phrased, or carry an explicit written justification — never silently accepted.
- CUE FRAME WINDOWS ARE MONOTONIC AND DISTINCT: startFrame/endFrame increase cue-over-cue with no two cues collapsed onto the same window and no overlap; each window plausibly contains its own words.
- CLIPS ARE TRIMMED TO REAL SPEECH: each narrationFrames equals the trimmed clip length (TTS lead-in/tail removed); neither padded with dead air nor clipped mid-word; values sane for the caption length at the calibrated rate.
- INTRA-CUE / INTER-CUE PAUSES ARE TYPED, NOT BAKED: any pause is a structured gap field with a reason, not silence baked inside a clip and not an in-text ellipsis; each clip is continuous speech.
- NO HELD-VOWEL DRONE OR RUN-ON: clean natural prosody — no stretched/droning vowel, no two cues' speech run together, no audible stitch artifact.
- OUTPUTS INTERNALLY CONSISTENT AND COMPLETE: clip count, lessonId, fps, sampleRate, cue id list agree across gemini-voice.json, asr-alignment.json, voice-clips.json; every cue has a clip file on disk and a phrase matching the configured ASR token pattern.
- A VERIFICATION AUDIT NOTE EXISTS: `_logs/voice.md` records the per-cue transcript-vs-script check, any re-rolls/edits, and the final accept decision — showing the audio was verified, not trusted, before freeze.

**Red flags**
- A TRUNCATED CLIP FROZEN INTO THE TIMELINE: a cue's clip cut mid-phrase (judged from the rendered WAV / its per-clip ASR coverage, not a model self-report) accepted and frozen — locks broken speech into every downstream wave. With the dedicated-TTS cure this should not occur; if the advisory canary fires on a genuinely cut clip, re-roll — never freeze it.
- STATUS LAUNDERED FROM THE BEST CUE: an overall ok hiding multiple low-evidence cues, or a matchScore far below threshold accepted with no re-roll and no justification.
- COLLAPSED / SHARED FRAME WINDOWS: several cues sharing one startFrame/endFrame — the aligner found no distinct evidence and the timing is fiction.
- BAKED DEAD-AIR OR BAKED PAUSE: silence padded inside a clip, or a learner-response pause encoded as in-clip silence instead of a typed gap.
- HELD-VOWEL DRONE / IN-TEXT ELLIPSIS producing a smeared, unnatural delivery.
- RUN-ON OR CROSS-CUE CLIP: one clip containing more than its own cue's narration (the desync class v4 per-cue clips exist to prevent).
- MISMATCHED OR MISSING OUTPUTS: clipCount/cue lists disagree, a referenced clip wav missing, or phrase tokens absent because the token pattern is wrong.
- RE-RECORDING FOR VISUAL FIT: any sign the voice was regenerated to fit motion after this node (fix is cut/compress motion at W4, never re-voice).

## W3b primitive gap-scan/build — `lesson-data/<id>/primitive-gap-scan.md` + (only when a gap is named) new family-file primitives, registry prose, `primitive-checks/*.png`

**Purpose.** Hands the composer a complete, verified REUSE/BUILD verdict per teaching-action demand — so every visual the lesson needs is either a named existing primitive or a newly-shipped, registered, aesthetically-verified one, with the intro card owned here, not improvised downstream.

**Acceptance criteria**

- Vague or missing verdict that forces W4 improvisation.

## W3.5 reconcile — `src/lessons/<camel>LessonTimeline.ts`

**Purpose.** The single source of truth for every cue's one shared window (start/end frames, narration sub-window, hold/gap), consumed identically by the composer (visuals), the audio layer (per-cue voice clips), and captions — so all three are frame-locked and cannot drift.

**Acceptance criteria**
- Cue windows are COMPUTED, not authored: the module imports the per-cue clip truth and a per-cue motion-budget map and calls the kit reconcile helper (reconcileClipTimeline). No cue start/end frame is hand-typed or copied from any other file.
- The reconcile formula is the v4 cue-anchored one verbatim: `cueFrames = max(narrationFrames + gapFrames, motionFrames) + tail`, cues chained end-to-end from frame 0, tail ≤ ~9 frames / 0.3s. The duration export equals the last cue's endFrame.
- EXACTLY ONE per-cue entry in each direction — parity is judged against the CLIP MODULE: every cue in the generated clip module appears in the visual-motion-budget map and the output cue list, matching IDs (a missing budget should throw, not silently default). visual-design may legitimately budget MORE beats than reach voice — W2b folds echo/wait beats into a typed gap on a carrying cue; those design-only rows get NO budget entry and their dwell must be covered by the carrying cue's gapFrames. Dropping them is a conscious, logged decision, never a silent omission.
- Three views derived from the SAME reconciled object — Cues, per-cue VoiceClips, CaptionCues all flow from one reconcile call; captions mapped from reconciled cues (not re-authored); each VoiceClip anchored to its own cue's startFrame for its measured narration length only.
- Narration audio treated as frozen input: narrationFrames come straight from the measured/trimmed clip module; the module reads them rather than re-deriving from ASR or raw WAVs. Any intra-cue pause is a typed gap.
- The motion budget per cue is a sensible floor sourced from visual-design's visualMotionSeconds (seconds), and where narration exceeds it the window stretches to fit the spoken span plus tail (max() actually binds, visible per cue).
- Self-documenting about inputs and discipline: a header names its two inputs (clip truth + visual-design budgets) and states the cue-as-unit law.
- Pure and deterministic: no Date.now/Math.random/fs/network, no per-lesson hardcoded topic strings or magic frame constants beyond FPS and the tail; same inputs → byte-identical output.

**Red flags**
- Re-introduces any banned legacy mechanism: a PADDED_CUE_DURATIONS_FRAMES table, a single continuous voice WAV/Sequence, detect-silences / ASR-boundary snapping, or a continuous-audio drift path.
- Cue start/end frames hand-typed, copy-pasted, or duplicated in a second file (composer/audio/captions computing their own boundaries).
- narrationFrames or cue lengths recomputed from ASR text, raw WAV duration, or audio-captions estimates instead of read from the frozen measured clip module.
- Formula deviates: tail folded INTO motionFrames or applied inconsistently, max() replaced by sum or narration-only/motion-only, gaps dropped, or cues not chained contiguously.
- Budget/clip mismatch passes silently — a cue missing from the budget map gets a hidden default, or a cue in one list absent from the other.
- Captions or VoiceClips authored independently of the reconciled cues (separate caption timing, voice clip fromFrame ≠ its cue startFrame, or durationInFrames padded beyond the measured clip).
- Lesson-specific contamination: topic words, Chinese strings, or specific frame totals baked into the reconcile logic.
- No tier-2 log (`_logs/w3-5-reconcile.md`) — the animatic-gate verdict and the comprehension-floor advisory result are unauditable without it.

## W4a composer — `<Pascal>LessonScene.tsx`, `<camel>/layout.ts`, `<camel>/manifest.ts`, `Complete<Pascal>Lesson.tsx`

**Purpose.** The renderable Remotion scene + its bbox manifest that turn the reconciled cue timeline and approved upstream artifacts into the actual moving picture W5 renders and W6 verifies.

**Acceptance criteria**
- Zero frame literals and zero raw motion literals: every absolute frame derives from `cues[id].startFrame/endFrame` + a NAMED layout.ts offset (offsets clamped against endFrame so motion never overruns), and every easing/spring is a named EASE.*/SPRING.* (or PopIn/Breathe) — no bare Easing.bezier numbers, no {damping,stiffness,mass} literals, no PADDED_CUE_DURATIONS_FRAMES.
- Spatially disjoint, render-verified layout: load-bearing elements CO-PRESENT on screen never overlap each other's drawn pixels (question/prompt text not on top of the teaching objects; split clusters gapped enough to read), CONFIRMED by actually rendering multiple stills (intro at full cast opacity, climax mid-frame, every peak-coexistence frame) and LOOKING at them — not inferred from JSON alone.
- Honors the reconciled timeline as-is: the scene only READS the cues/voiceClips/duration the timeline exports; when motion overruns a cue it trims non-load-bearing flourishes then compresses uniformly, never extends the cue, never re-pads, never re-triggers voice. durationInFrames comes from the timeline module, not a literal.
- Identity invariants enforced structurally: the teaching unit persists as ONE primitive instance across the transformation (positions/count/opacity interpolate; not destroyed and recreated per cue), color/size/type constant.
- On-screen target strings are a subset of the CURRENT cue's own spoken phrase, in spoken order (derived from that cue's script-cues.json text), so the child never reads a word this cue's audio does not speak.
- Intro is choreographed, not piled: the title resolves and is readable on its own before the teaching cast enters (or the cast lives in a band that never overlaps the title).
- Manifest fidelity: every load-bearing element in the Visual Contract registered with a bboxAt using the SAME layout.ts constants and interpolation as the scene; the scene tags each with matching measureProps('<id>') and calls useMeasureHook() once; `lesson:check --measured` was run with collisionCount/measuredCollisionCount/gatesFailed all clean OR every nonzero result justified in writing after opening the flagged frame.
- Composition lint-clean and self-contained: PascalCase scene/wrapper components, ESM imports only, no unused vars, theme tokens (no hardcoded hex) and named curves throughout; registers via its OWN Complete*Lesson.tsx descriptor with no edits to Root.tsx/Composition.tsx or any primitive file; bed wired mechanically and SFX fire at the composer's own cue-relative motion frames.

**Red flags**
- Self-graded GREEN from the bbox JSON / a measured-pass 0-collision report without ever rendering and visually inspecting a frame — the eye contradicts the numbers (the canonical sandbox-EPERM "I couldn't render so I trusted the JSON" miss).
- Overlapping zones used as a substitute for spatial separation: manifest zone rectangles intersect in coordinate space and the scene relies only on elements being temporally non-co-present, so any drift puts question/label text ON the teaching objects (the learner-gap text landing on the dots).
- A split/cluster gap (or teaching-unit size) too small to read at real render size — passes a loose bbox envelope but fails the finger-cover / kid-eye legibility test.
- Frame or motion literals leak in: a bare number, an inline Easing.bezier/spring literal, a re-introduced hold/padding table, or a cue extended/padded to fit overrunning motion.
- The scene re-authors upstream truth: new pedagogy/copy/cues not in script-cues.json, an on-screen word the current cue's audio never speaks, or re-generated/re-trimmed voice.
- Manifest drift or gaps: a load-bearing element missing from the manifest, or a bboxAt computed from different constants/easing than the scene; missing/mismatched measureProps ids.
- Intro title-occlusion: title and cast rendered at the same instant in overlapping positions.
- Shared-file or primitive edits: touching Root.tsx/Composition.tsx, modifying a shape-primitive instead of kicking back to Wave 3, or hardcoding hex; or dead air in a learner-response gap (a bare low-opacity glow with no readable label/glyph).
- **Vacuous green** — a 0-collision report that never compared the failing pair: a blanket zone-pair exemption (or a missing manifest entry, or a wrong zone tag) swallows exactly the overlap on screen. A green count is only meaningful if the pair is actually checked — verify WHAT the gate compared, not just its number.
- **Justification laundering** — a collision/gate-failure "justification" that names an element, frame, or cause not present in the failing JSON row (e.g. attributing a contrast FAIL to a different element's intentional dimming).
- **Per-cue re-entrance blink** — the persistent teaching unit re-enters from opacity 0 inside each cue (motion-window fade-in), so the canvas is empty under live narration before every motion phase; often paired with a manifest that reports opacity 1 for the same frames.

## W4b sketch overlay — `lesson-data/<id>/sketch-overlay.md`

**Purpose.** The cue-relative spec for hand-drawn teacher-mark overlays the composer turns into `<TeacherMark>` instances + manifest entries — WHICH cue gets a mark, WHAT it points at, and WHEN, anchored to cue boundaries so it survives ASR re-timing.

**Acceptance criteria**
- RESTRAINT IS HONORED: total marks ≤ floor(cueCount × 0.6), most cues carry ZERO marks. A good artifact is far below the ceiling (one or a few marks). Every no-mark cue gives a 1-line reason naming the element that already carries that cue's signal.
- EVERY MARK EARNS ITS SLOT: each completes the "carries signal X, not already carried by Y or Z" sentence with a real, cue-specific signal — not generic decoration; tied to the cue's pedagogical beat (the NEW property or the visual climax).
- CUE-RELATIVE FRAMES ONLY: every mark's timing is offsets from a named cueId (drawOnRelativeStart / drawOnDuration / fadeOutRelativeStart), ZERO master-timeline absolutes; reveals via stroke draw-on (never fade-in) and fades out before the cue ends.
- MARK POINTS AT WHAT IT EMPHASIZES: each anchor span/endpoints are tight to the specific referent — not a wide stroke spanning everything; co-timed with the climax primitive (lands on the settled state, not mid-motion).
- VOCABULARY + ZONE DISCIPLINE: only an authorized kind (underline / wrap-arc / label-arrow / vs-mark) — no invented circles/ticks/sparkles; anchors may trace zone-objects but NEVER sit inside zone-labels; label-arrow ends explicit; the kind chosen is justified over alternatives.
- ANCHORS RESOLVABLE WITHOUT LITERALS: endpoints are named element ids (or coords) the composer resolves against live geometry at build time.
- SKETCH LANGUAGE SPECIFIED ONCE AND CONSISTENT: a single ink color from theme tokens, stroke width, opacity, jitter declared up front; opt-in boil/settle only within their gates (boil only on a long-held decorative mark, settle climax-only, never on the teaching primitive) with the reasoning shown.
- COMPOSER HAND-OFF COMPLETE: the `<TeacherMark>` instance shape, the cue→real-frame resolution math (clamped to cue end), and a required manifest "marks" entry (bboxAt = padded anchor span, opacityAt = the draw-on × fade-out math) so lesson:check can see the mark and flag collisions. Fade-out is SEMANTICALLY end-anchored — accept `fadeOutRelativeStart` only when stated as the expression `cueLength − 8` (or an explicit end-anchored field), never a resolved literal, so the value survives re-timing.

**Red flags**
- A climax/emphasis mark whose span covers the whole scene or every countable object, so it emphasizes nothing — e.g. one underline stretched under all items (the real post-mortem failure) — UNLESS the cue's discovery IS a relation over exactly that span (e.g. an underline binding two clusters the cue teaches as an equal pair, prescribed by the Visual Contract). The flag is the indiscriminate sweep whose span is not the discovery's referent.
- Mark count at or above the ceiling, or a mark on most/every cue; a verbose mark table padded to look thorough.
- Any absolute master-timeline frame number instead of a cueId-relative offset.
- A mark anchored inside zone-labels, or anchors given as hand-coded x/y literals the composer can't re-resolve.
- Invented mark types/glyphs outside the authorized vocabulary.
- Boil or settle misused (boil on a barely-held mark or the teaching primitive, settle on a short glance mark, multiple boiled/settled marks) without justification.
- fade-in reveal instead of stroke draw-on, or a mark that bleeds into the next cue.
- Missing/hand-wavy composer hand-off: no manifest "marks" entry, or no clamped cue→frame resolution math.
- No-mark cues left blank with no reason, or reasons that don't name the element already carrying the signal.

## W5 render — `out/<id>/<id>.mp4`, `<id>-contact.png` (+ `.json`), `bbox-manifest.json`

**Purpose.** The finished, broadcast-loudness lesson master plus the single-image contact sheet that W6 verification (and the human) reviews to judge whether the lesson actually teaches.

**Acceptance criteria**
- MP4 is a valid, complete, playable file: H.264 video + AAC audio muxed, 1280×720 at 30fps, frame count = the reconciled total duration (no truncated/zero-length file, no missing audio track).
- Video and audio durations agree with each other and with the reconciled timeline (contact.json totalDuration / fps): no audio ending early or running long past the picture.
- Loudness normalized to spec: integrated loudness near −16 LUFS and true peak ≤ −1 dBTP (re-measuring lands within tolerance, not clipped, not far quieter/louder). **The loudness claim quotes the FILE's measured `input_i`/`input_tp` from a re-measure (or the script's printed `Loudnorm verified` line) — a loudnorm print JSON's `output_*` fields describe a hypothetical further pass, never the file.**
- The contact sheet PNG actually exists and renders as a real image (non-trivial file size, not blank/black/error) — the mandatory human review surface; its absence is a render failure, not a skipped nicety.
- Contact sheet legible and complete: one row per cue in order, each labeled with cue ID + frame range, with the agreed samples per cue (start / narr-mid / narr-end / hold-mid / cue-end) so stagnation and dead holds are visible at a glance.
- Contact sheet content matches the master: sampled frames look like real rendered lesson frames (correct background, primitives present, captions where expected), the same frames the MP4 plays — not stale, not from a different lesson, not all-identical.
- Artifacts internally consistent: contact.json fps/totalDuration/cue boundaries agree with the MP4 probe and the upstream reconcile; bbox-manifest summary blocks present (linear collisionCount + measured block) for verification cross-check.

**Red flags**
- MP4 missing, zero-byte, unplayable, or no audio/video stream; frame count or duration far shorter than the reconciled timeline (silent truncation).
- Wrong mechanical container: not 1280×720, not 30fps, not H.264+AAC.
- Loudness off-target: un-normalized, clipping above −1 dBTP, or markedly off −16 LUFS (loudnorm silently skipped without a printed SKIP reason).
- Contact sheet did NOT render — e.g. a sandbox/permission (EPERM) error on the still-frame path; a real defect to flag and fix, never wave through as "skipped".
- Contact sheet rendered but unusable: blank/black tiles, error placeholders, missing rows, wrong sample count, unlabeled cues, or too cramped/low-res to judge teaching.
- Contact sheet stale or mismatched: a previous render, a different lesson, or frames not matching what the MP4 plays.
- Audio/picture desync in the master: voice clip leads/lags its cue's visuals (a regression of the continuous-WAV drift), or a clip bleeds across a cue boundary.
- Artifacts disagree: contact.json / bbox-manifest report a duration, fps, or cue boundaries that don't match the actual MP4.
- Loudness self-report quotes loudnorm `output_*` (telltale: the quoted LRA/thresh match the `output_lra`/`output_thresh` block) while the file's `input_*` measures differently — a false "in spec" claim.
